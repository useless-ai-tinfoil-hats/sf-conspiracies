California voters will now choose ‘keep the law’ or ‘overturn the law’ when voting on referendums
-----
When opponents of a state law qualify a referendum for the ballot, voters must decide whether to oppose the referendum — which means voting "yes" on the ballot measure — or vice versa, a format that is potentially confusing. That's about to change.Under AB421, signed by Gov. Gavin Newsom on Sunday, when a referendum challenging a proposed state law appears on the ballot, voters will be told they have two choices: "Keep the law" or "Overturn the law."
