The California botanic garden with a trashy past
-----
The first hint of the South Coast Botanic Garden's trashy history is the long and bumpy driveway that leads into an 87-acre garden.The road is bumpy because it — and the entire botanic garden, set against the backdrop of the Pacific Ocean on the Palos Verdes peninsula south of Los Angeles — sits atop a former landfill. As the trash beneath the earth continues to shift and compact, it causes bumps and divots in the path.
