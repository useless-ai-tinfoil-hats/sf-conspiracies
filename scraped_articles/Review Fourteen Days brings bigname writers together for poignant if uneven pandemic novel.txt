Review: 'Fourteen Days' brings big-name writers together for poignant if uneven pandemic novel
-----
Getting 36 American and Canadian authors of different social, cultural, political and religious backgrounds together to write one novel is no easy feat.But if there's one current event that can bring everyone together to work on a creative endeavor, it's COVID-19. In this case, the project is "Fourteen Days," a poignant and emotionally resonant yet slightly repetitive and uneven collaborative novel edited by Margaret Atwood and Douglas Preston.
