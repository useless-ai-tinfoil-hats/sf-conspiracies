Oakland group that lost WNBA bid to Warriors' Lacob says league didn't let them apply
-----
The WNBA's official arrival in San Francisco with a Warriors-owned team has been celebrated by most of the Bay Area.But that hasn't been the case for Oakland and the African American Sports & Entertainment Group (AASEG). The group had been publicly pursuing a WNBA team for its city for more than two years, even though AASEG founder Ray Bobbitt admitted that he knew it would be tough to beat out the bid from the Warriors' brass.
