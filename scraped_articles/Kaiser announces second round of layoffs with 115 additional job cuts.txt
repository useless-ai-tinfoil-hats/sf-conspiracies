Kaiser announces second round of layoffs, with 115 additional job cuts
-----
Kaiser Permanente plans to reduce its workforce by 115 positions across the country in its second wave of layoffs this fall, primarily affecting IT workers in Northern California.According to filings submitted to the state in early November, the health care giant said it will eliminate 41 positions in its Pleasanton office, a dozen across three Oakland locations, and one each in its South San Francisco and Walnut Creek offices before the end of the year, totaling 65 cuts in the region.
