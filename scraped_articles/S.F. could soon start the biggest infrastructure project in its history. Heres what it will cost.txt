S.F. could soon start the biggest infrastructure project in its history. Here’s what it will cost
-----
San Francisco and the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers could soon embark on the largest infrastructure project in the city's history — preparing the bay shoreline for sea level rise, an endeavor that could last decades and cost more than $10 billion.Four years after studies began, the Port of San Francisco is poised to sign off on the Army Corps' release of a draft plan on how best to protect 7.5 miles of urban waterfront from the potential impact of climate change.
