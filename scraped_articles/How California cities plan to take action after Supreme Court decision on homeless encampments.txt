How California cities plan to take action after Supreme Court decision on homeless encampments
-----
For years, cities in California and across the West have been asking for more leeway when it comes to addressing homelessness. The Supreme Court granted their wish Friday, when it ruled on a case from Grants Pass, Ore., that gives cities broad power to evict people from street encampments and confiscate their property.So what will cities start to do differently?
It could take a while to get a full picture of how individual cities' policies will change. But some have already begun
