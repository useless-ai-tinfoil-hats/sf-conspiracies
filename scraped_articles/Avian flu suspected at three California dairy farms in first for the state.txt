Avian flu suspected at three California dairy farms in first for the state
-----
California agriculture officials are investigating the possibility that H5N1 avian flu has infected cattle in three Central Valley dairy herds, the state Department of Food and Agriculture said Thursday.If the virus is confirmed, it would be the first cases of H5N1 in dairy cows in California. Thousands of birds in the state have been infected since the current strain began circulating in 2022, but the virus has yet to be detected in dairy cows here.
