UC Berkeley housing crunch: Building heights could rise to create thousands of new units
-----
Berkeley City Council will soon consider whether to allow taller buildings in a densely populated neighborhood near UC Berkeley, the first of several steps the city is considering to ease its housing crunch.The Planning Commission voted unanimously Wednesday night to approve changes that would allow developers to build up to 12-story apartment buildings and ease some development regulations in the Southside neighborhood adjacent to the university.
