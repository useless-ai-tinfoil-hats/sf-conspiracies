Bay Area biotech firm lays off dozens more scientists just months after 436-worker cut
-----
Genentech, a drugmaker based in South San Francisco, is laying off 93 more local workers just months after a 436-worker cut.The company, which is owned by Swiss pharmaceutical giant Roche, announced the new layoffs in a Thursday WARN document, as is generally required in the event of mass layoffs. Scientist roles will be the hardest hit, per the filing, but the layoff round will also include engineers, managers, analysts and one vice president.
