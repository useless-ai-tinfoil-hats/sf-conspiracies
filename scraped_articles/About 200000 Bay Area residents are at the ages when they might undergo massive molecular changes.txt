About 200,000 Bay Area residents are at the ages when they might undergo massive molecular changes
-----
Aging doesn't occur steadily, according to a new study led by Stanford researchers. Instead the human body ages dramatically in two bursts, around age 44 and then again around age 60, based on analyses of thousands of biological molecules.In the Bay Area, there are around 200,000 residents in those two phases, according to projections by the California Department of Finance.
