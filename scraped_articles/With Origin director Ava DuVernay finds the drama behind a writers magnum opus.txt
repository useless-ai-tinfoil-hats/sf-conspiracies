With ‘Origin,’ director Ava DuVernay finds the drama behind a writer’s magnum opus
-----
Isabel Wilkerson's "Caste: The Origins of Our Discontent" is a scholarly work of nonfiction, without characters or plot, in which the journalist and author argues that racism in the United States is just one piece in a caste system. It is a globe-trotting work in which Wilkerson compares American hierarchies to those in India and Nazi Germany, and yet, it is not a work most people would associate with drama.But writer-director Ava DuVernay is not most people.
