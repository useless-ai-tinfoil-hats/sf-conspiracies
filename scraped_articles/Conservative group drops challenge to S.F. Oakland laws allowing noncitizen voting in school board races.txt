Conservative group drops challenge to S.F., Oakland laws allowing noncitizen voting in school board races
-----
A court ruling allowing noncitizen parents in San Francisco to vote in local school board elections became final Tuesday when the leader of a conservative nonprofit said he wouldn't appeal it.The state's First District Court of Appeal ruled in August that a ballot measure approved by San Francisco voters in 2016, the first of its kind in the state, did not violate a long-standing provision of the California Constitution that says U.S. citizens aged 18 and older "may vote" but is silent
