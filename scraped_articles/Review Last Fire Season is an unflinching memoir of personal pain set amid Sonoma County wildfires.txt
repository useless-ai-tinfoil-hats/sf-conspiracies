Review: ‘Last Fire Season’ is an unflinching memoir of personal pain set amid Sonoma County wildfires
-----
Manjula Martin's beautifully written new memoir revisits one of the grimmer periods in recent memory, a four-month span in 2020 when Northern California wildfires, in conspiracy with COVID-19, made it feel like "everywhere was dangerous, inside and out," she writes.To its credit, "The Last Fire Season: A Personal and Pyronatural History" isn't trying to land on anyone's feel-good book-of-the-month-club list.
