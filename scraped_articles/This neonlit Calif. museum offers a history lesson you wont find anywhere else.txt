This neon-lit Calif. museum offers a history lesson you won't find anywhere else
-----
Rays of neon light seep under the doors at the Valley Relics Museum in Los Angeles, housed in a duo of corrugated steel airplane hangars in the middle of the San Fernando Valley. The glow can be blinding at first, but there is a point to all this wattage: Owner Tommy Gelinas loves the sprawling, misunderstood Valley, and turning on these lights every weekend for paying customers is his way of illuminating the region's dimming history."I love everything about the Valley," Gelinas told
