S.F.’s school board could finally focus on kids — if another political war doesn’t break out first
-----
It is not hyperbole to suggest that the futures of the San Francisco Unified School District and the nearly 50,000 kids it serves are currently on the line.Foreshadowing a potential state takeover, California officials recently gave fiscal appointees veto power over the district's spending, citing its $421 million budget shortfall and poor financial decision-making. Declining enrollment and dwindling coffers are forcing the district to prepare to close or merge some of its 100 schools.
