Mysterious formations cover the seafloor off California coast. Scientists are closer to knowing why
-----
About 25 miles off the coast from Big Sur, more than 5,200 giant depressions decorate a stretch of seafloor roughly 10 times the size of San Francisco. Each of the circular formations spans the length of about two football fields and gradually slopes down about 16 feet.The structures, known as pockmarks, are spaced out fairly regularly, separated by an average of about 500 feet.
