Why experts say ‘everyone’ should carry Narcan, the powerful opioid-reversal medication
-----
Dr. Dan Ciccarone, a professor of addiction medicine at UCSF, carries it in his backpack wherever he goes.Dr. Beth Mulcahy, an emergency physician at Kaiser in San Francisco, has it in her car. Her teenagers carry it in their backpacks.
With the opioid overdose-reversing nasal spray Narcan poised to hit pharmacy shelves this month as an over-the-counter product for the first time, experts like Ciccarone and Mulcahy are urging the public to make Narcan a medicine cabinet staple.
