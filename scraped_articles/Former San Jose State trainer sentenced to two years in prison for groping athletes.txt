Former San Jose State trainer sentenced to two years in prison for groping athletes
-----
San Jose State's former chief of sports medicine, Scott Shaw, has been sentenced to two years in federal prison after admitting that he had touched the breasts and buttocks of four female athletes whom he was supposed to be treating for injuries.Shaw, now 56, was the school's chief athletic trainer and sports medicine director from 2008 until August 2020. He pleaded guilty this August to two charges of violating the four athletes' rights in incidents between 2017 and 2020.
