Review: ‘The 39 Steps’ demonstrates the peril of conservative programming
-----
Amid drawn-out pandemic recovery, one strategy theaters have adopted is to program more conservatively, opting for recognizable titles that might spur even the most recalcitrant butts out of the COVID-honed sofa groove."The 39 Steps" is a case in point. Even some non-theater fans have likely heard of Alfred Hitchcock's spy caper, and Patrick Barlow's stage adaptation, which gets mounted all the time, is renowned for its theatrical hijinks.
