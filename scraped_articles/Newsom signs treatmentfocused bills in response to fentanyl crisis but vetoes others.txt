Newsom signs treatment-focused bills in response to fentanyl crisis, but vetoes others
-----
Gov. Gavin Newsom pointed to budget constraints and his administration's existing opioid work in vetoing several bills to combat California's fentanyl crisis, even as he signed others that aim to make addiction and overdose drugs more widely available.The state will require stadiums, concert venues and amusement parks to stock doses of opioid overdose reversal drugs under one new law Newsom signed.
