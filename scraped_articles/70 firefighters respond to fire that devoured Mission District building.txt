70 firefighters respond to fire that devoured Mission District building
-----
A fire broke out in an unoccupied commercial building in San Francisco's Mission District early Thursday, with 70 firefighters rushing to the scene to extinguish flames, fire officials said.The fire ignited just after midnight in a building at 2975 Mission St. (on the block of Mission between 25th and 26th streets) and was under control by 12:59 a.m., according to Capt. Jonathan Baxter, a spokesperson with the San Francisco Fire Department.
