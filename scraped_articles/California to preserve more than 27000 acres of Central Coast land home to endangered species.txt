California to preserve more than 27,000 acres of Central Coast land, home to endangered species
-----
Last week, California's Wildlife Conservation Board earmarked more than $10 million to preserve a 27,000-acre parcel of land on the Central Coast.In a quarterly meeting held Feb. 15, the board awarded a $10.3 million grant to the Land Conservancy of San Luis Obispo County. The nonprofit group will use the money to protect the Camatta Ranch in Santa Margarita, a family-owned cattle ranch comparable in land area to the entire city of San Francisco.
