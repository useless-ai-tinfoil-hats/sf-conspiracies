'Robust aftershock sequence' follows magnitude 5.2 quake in Southern California
-----
A magnitude 5.2 earthquake rumbled through Southern California on Tuesday night, sending tremors across the Los Angeles Basin and triggering dozens of aftershocks, according to the U.S. Geological Survey.The main quake hit at 9:09 p.m. with a depth of 7 miles in a remote area of Kern County, about 17 miles southwest of Bakersfield. Shaking was felt as far north as Fresno, as far south as Long Beach and as far east as the Inland Empire, according to the USGS.
