Review: Lamplighters put a Mexican spin on a Gilbert and Sullivan favorite
-----
Midway through the second act of Gilbert and Sullivan's comic operetta "Ruddygore," a passel of the main character's aristocratic forebears come mysteriously to life. These ancient nobles, dating back several centuries, emerge from their musty oil portraits in the ancestral mansion to enforce certain dark family traditions.David Euresti, the director of the delightful new production that opened Saturday, Feb. 24, and continues at Lamplighters Music Theatre, must have pondered that moment
