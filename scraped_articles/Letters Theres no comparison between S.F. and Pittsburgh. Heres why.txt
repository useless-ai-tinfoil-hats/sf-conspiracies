Letters: There’s no comparison between S.F. and Pittsburgh. Here’s why
-----
Regarding "What San Francisco can learn from the collapse — and rebirth — of one U.S. city" (San Francisco, SFChronicle.com, Sept. 6): Comparing our city to Pittsburgh just perpetuates the recent doom and gloom messaging about the future of San Francisco.Yes, Pittsburgh has shed itself of its industrial past, reinventing itself into a small knowledge-based city of 300,000, though with half the population it had 70 years ago.
