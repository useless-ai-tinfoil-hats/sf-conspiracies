The H-1B visa is virtually the only legal way for international students or foreign nationals to work in the United States long-term, but obtaining it requires aspiring immigrants to, quite literally, win the lottery.Ahead of the annual lottery season beginning March 6, a new report by the National Foundation for American Policy, a nonpartisan think tank based in Arlington, Va., found the odds of any H-1B hopeful winning the visa lottery have only gotten worse in the past three years.
