New West Napa fault maps show threat could be greater than previously thought
-----
Mapping research conducted by the U.S. Geological Survey recently revealed that the West Napa fault line, which was responsible for the 6.0 South Napa earthquake in 2014, is miles longer and significantly stronger than it was originally thought to be.At the time of the 2014 quake, the fault was believed to stretch from northern Vallejo to the city of St. Helena.
