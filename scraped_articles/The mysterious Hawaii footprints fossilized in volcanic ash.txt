The mysterious Hawaii footprints fossilized in volcanic ash
-----
On Hawaii Island, the Kau Desert on the southwest side of Kilauea Volcano is a dry, desolate landscape. Life is hindered by the frequency of acid rain, and layers of ash cover the vast, moonlike environment from centuries of eruptions.Among the hardened lava, and dusted with sand, is a violent reminder of the volcano's unpredictability: 1,773 human footprints fossilized in the ash, representing at least 441 men, women and children.
