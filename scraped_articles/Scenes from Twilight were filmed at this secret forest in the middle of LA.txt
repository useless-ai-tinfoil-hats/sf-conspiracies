There is a specific look to the backdrop of Los Angeles, a kind of arid rolling landscape dotted by palm trees, that can often give the incorrect impression that the state's largest city is built on a desert (it isn't). Most of the vegetation at Griffith Park, among the nation's biggest municipal parks and located right in the heart of the city, looks pretty standard for Southern California, with natural chaparral across the hillsides.
