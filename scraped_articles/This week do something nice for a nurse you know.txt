The Greeks maintained the legend of Hygieia, the Greek goddess of health, the first nurse and a daughter of Asclepius. She oversaw cleanliness and preventive medicine.Our modern understanding starts with Florence Nightingale, who led 38 nurses to Crimea in October 1854 to care for British soldiers.
