Trendy tinned fish is mostly imported. Can this S.F. maker change that?
-----
Deep in the pandemic lockdown, when baking sourdough bread became a household hobby, Stephanie Fels was consumed by a more unusual culinary pursuit: tinned fish.Her one-bedroom apartment in New York City was overtaken by cans, equipment and the smell of seafood. She used a pressure cooker to safely can fish, from trout and salmon to striped sea bass and halibut. (She put herself through a seafood food safety certification program beforehand.)
