California's new $20 million train is unlike anything else in the US
-----
In a win for California's race to hit its climate mandates and reach net-zero carbon emissions by 2045, the state unveiled its latest addition to its railroad infrastructure.On Thursday, the San Bernardino County Transportation Authority unveiled its Zero Emission Multiple Unit, also known as ZEMU, at the San Bernardino Depot Train Station, according to a news release.
ZEMU trains only emit water vapor and operate using a "hybrid hydrogen and battery technology to propel the train
