In ‘Bushman,’ a glimpse at a San Francisco that has vanished, and filmmaker who observed it
-----
San Francisco's Fillmore neighborhood, as Chronicle columnist Justin Phillips recently observed while writing about the controversial imminent closure of a Safeway there, was once called the "Harlem of the West."That's hard to believe today. But proof that the Fillmore was a mecca to low-income Black populations exists in a remarkable, newly restored film from 1971 called "Bushman," directed by David Schickele.
