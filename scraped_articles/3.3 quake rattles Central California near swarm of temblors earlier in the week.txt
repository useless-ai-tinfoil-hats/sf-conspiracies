3.3 quake rattles Central California near swarm of temblors earlier in the week
-----
A 3.3 magnitude earthquake struck about 10 miles southwest of Patterson (Stanislaus County) at about 9 a.m. Saturday, near a swarm of quakes that struck earlier in the week, according to the U.S. Geological Survey.Reports of damage in the Central California community were not immediately available.
A 4.5 quake struck near Westley on Monday night, Stanislaus County's largest earthquake on record, part of a swarm of more than 10 earthquakes.
