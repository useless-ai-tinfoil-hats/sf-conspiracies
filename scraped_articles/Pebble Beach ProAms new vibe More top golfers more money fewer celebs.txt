Pebble Beach Pro-Am’s new vibe: More top golfers, more money, fewer celebs
-----
This once counted as a time-honored ritual ahead of the AT&T Pebble Beach Pro-Am: scanning the professional field, trying to find the two or three golfers ranked among the world's best.It's a whole new deal this year.
The onetime Crosby Clambake, which long ago carved out a distinctive identity on the Northern California sports landscape — personified by actor/comedian Bill Murray's antics on the links — is changing colors, at least in 2024. Now it's mostly about the golf.
