They hit the S.F. housing jackpot. But the 33 Tehama flooding turned it into a nightmare
-----
Mousa Mihtar hit the San Francisco housing jackpot in 2018, when he clinched a one-bedroom apartment in a luxury tower at 33 Tehama Street.It was only a couple miles from a shabby studio in the Tenderloin that Mihtar had shared with his wife and children. But the distance felt like so much more. Swathed in glass, the giant 33 Tehama high-rise represented a part of downtown that had become a nesting ground for tech workers. Residents lived in spacious units with Italian craft furniture
