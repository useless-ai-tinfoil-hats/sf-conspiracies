Crippled by a seemingly endless pandemic hangover and a lack of investor confidence in the city's future, 2023 was San Francisco's weakest year for housing production in a decade, with just 2,024 units completed.The crop of new units represented a 30% decline from 2022, and a 57% drop from 2021. Among the 2,024 new apartments and condos were 805 affordable units, which was 37% less than the prior year.
