Review: Relevant, ambitious ‘Manahatta’ sags under weight of its scaffolding
-----
Big ideas about consequential events and issues in American life jockey to be heard in the daunting drama "Manahatta."In scenes that shuttle from the 17th century Dutch colonization of the Lenape-occupied island (later named Manhattan) to the financial collapse of 2008, playwright Mary Kathryn Nagle uses the long lens of history to compare and conflate notions of capital and ownership, language and Indigenous identity, ambition and deracination, money and manipulation.
