Neighbors in San Joaquin Valley unite to treat wild peacock that was shot with an arrow
-----
Residents of a rural neighborhood in California's San Joaquin Valley united to treat and rehabilitate a wild peacock that was shot with an arrow, according to the Washington Post.The outlet said the bird, now nicknamed Cupid, is one of about 20 wild peacocks that frequent the neighborhood near Exeter, a small city in Tulare County not far from the foothills of the Sierra Nevada.
