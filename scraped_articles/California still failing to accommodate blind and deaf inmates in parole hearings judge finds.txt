California still failing to accommodate blind and deaf inmates in parole hearings, judge finds
-----
A federal judge said California prison officials are not fully complying with her orders to provide deaf and blind with the materials and assistance they need to participate fully in parole hearings.U.S. District Judge Claudia Wilken of Oakland ruled in March that prison and parole board officials had failed to provide equipment and support to inmates with hearing and vision disabilities that would allow them to adequately prepare for and take part in parole hearings and appeal
