S.F. public hospital nurses reach tentative agreement to avert looming strike
-----
San Francisco public nurses reached a tentative agreement with the city Tuesday, potentially ending the threat of a strike authorized just days ago over staffing shortages and unsafe conditions for patients at the city's public hospital and clinics.The union representing the nurses, SEIU Local 1021, said the nurses secured improvements in several areas, including retention and recruitment, safety, and reduction in the use of contractors. The proposed deal would also narrow the wage gap
