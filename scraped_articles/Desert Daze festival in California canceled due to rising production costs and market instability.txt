Desert Daze festival in California canceled due to ‘rising production costs’ and market instability
-----
Organizers of Desert Daze, a popular psychedelic and underground rock festival in Southern California, have called off this year's event.Scheduled for Oct. 10-13 at Lake Perris in Riverside County, the festival was set to feature high-profile acts such as Jack White, Cigarettes After Sex and the Mars Volta, who performed at Golden Gate Park earlier this month. However, "rising production costs" and a "volatile festival market" have forced promoters to cancel.
