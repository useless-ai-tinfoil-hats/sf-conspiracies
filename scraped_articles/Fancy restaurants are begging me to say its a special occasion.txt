Fancy restaurants are begging me to say it’s a special occasion
-----
Whenever I went out to eat in my late teens and early 20s, it was always someone's "birthday." Why would you pass up a chance for a little extra something — a candle, a free dessert, an opportunity to embarrass an introverted friend?As a restaurant critic, I run away from special treatment. Do not perceive me, please, at least not any more than you would another chatty, somewhat nosy patron. I didn't even mention it was my birthday on my actual birthday, when I went out to a $$$ omakase
