NorCal tribe to take back 125 acres, create new national park entrance
-----
On Tuesday morning, a first-of-its-kind land deal paved the way for an important parcel of redwood forest to return to its original owner — California's largest Indigenous tribe — and to become a gateway to one of the state's nine national parks.Just outside of Orick, about 300 miles north of San Francisco, the 125-acre parcel is part of the Yurok Tribe's ancestral territory, 90% of which was forcibly taken by white settlers in the mid-1800s.
