S.F. officials push for homeless housing with a novel rule: No drug use
-----
It's been nearly three years since Danica Gutierrez had a sip of alcohol, but maintaining her sobriety and caring for her three children while living among others struggling with drug and alcohol addiction isn't easy.Gutierrez, 29, lives with her three children in one of San Francisco's permanent supportive housing sites for formerly homeless people in the South of Market district. Every day she says she shares an elevator with neighbors visibly under the influence of drugs or alcohol.
