California Citrus State Historic Park is an overlooked roadside attraction
-----
One of California's most important historic landmarks is stuck on the corner of a busy intersection, hidden beneath a large mesh screen with iron gates surrounding all sides.Two of the first navel orange trees in the United States were planted in Riverside in 1873, setting off what is sometimes called the "second California Gold Rush," but only this one lonely streetside tree remains at this place hardly worth visiting. Every single navel orange tree in the country is a descendant of one
