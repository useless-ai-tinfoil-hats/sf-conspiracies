Barbara Joans, anthropologist who studied women riding Harley-Davidson motorcycles, dies at 89
-----
Barbara Joans' motivation in moving from the back seat of her husband's Harley-Davidson to the pilot seat of her own Low Rider was academic. She was a cultural anthropologist interested in the sensation of screwing back the throttle and letting all that power roar out from under her.What Joans discovered in her study became the book "Bike Lust: Harleys, Women, and American Society," published by the University of Wisconsin Press in 2001, when she was 66 and had more years of open road
