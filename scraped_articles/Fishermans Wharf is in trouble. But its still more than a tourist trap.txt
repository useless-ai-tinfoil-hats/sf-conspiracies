Fisherman’s Wharf is in trouble. But it’s still more than a tourist trap
-----
The view east from Al Scoma Way distills the allure and afflictions of San Francisco's Fisherman's Wharf into one vivid tableau.Directly in front of you is the wharf's inner lagoon, where small fishing boats sway in the water. Behind them are stocky buildings that, for generations, housed such multilevel family-owned restaurants as Alioto's and Tarantino's — both of which sit vacant.
