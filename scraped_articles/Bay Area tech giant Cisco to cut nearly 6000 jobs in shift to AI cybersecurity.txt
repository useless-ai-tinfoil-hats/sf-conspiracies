Bay Area tech giant Cisco to cut nearly 6,000 jobs in shift to AI, cybersecurity
-----
In its second major round of layoffs this year, Bay Area tech giant Cisco Systems disclosed plans to cut an additional 7% of its global workforce on Wednesday as it shifts its focus toward artificial intelligence and cybersecurity.Although the San Jose company did not specify the number of jobs to be eliminated, Cisco employed 84,900 people last year before the initial cuts. In February, it announced plans to eliminate more than 4,200 positions as part of a broader reorganization.
