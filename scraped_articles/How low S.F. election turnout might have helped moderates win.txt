How low S.F. election turnout might have helped moderates win
-----
Tuesday's election was a gut punch to San Francisco progressives after voters embraced several ballot measures backed by moderates and they likely lost control of a key Democratic Party organization. But turnout may have played a role in their defeats and the victories of moderate candidates and ballot measures, experts say.Turnout is expected to be about 43%, according to a Wednesday projection by the Department of Elections. The department said it has counted nearly all of the ballots
