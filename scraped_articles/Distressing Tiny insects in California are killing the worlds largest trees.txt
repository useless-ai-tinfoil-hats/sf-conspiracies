'Distressing': Tiny insects in California are killing the world's largest trees
-----
It's not often one breathes a sigh of relief when suspended 275 feet in the air.That's precisely what Anthony Ambrose, executive director of the Ancient Forest Society, did when he reached the canopy of the General Sherman Tree. He and four others — the first known climbers to ascend the world's largest tree — were searching for bark beetles.
