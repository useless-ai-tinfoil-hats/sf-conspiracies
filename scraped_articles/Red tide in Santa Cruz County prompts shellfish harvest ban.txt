Red tide in Santa Cruz County prompts shellfish harvest ban
-----
A red tide has appeared on Santa Cruz County beaches, which indicates a harmful algal bloom that produces toxins in the waters.Algal blooms, which often turn the water red, have appeared at the Rio Del Mar and Seacliff beaches as of last Wednesday, Eric Baugher, a Santa Cruz water quality specialist, told SFGATE.
Red tides in Santa Cruz aren't common, and it's not known what triggers the algal blooms, he said.
