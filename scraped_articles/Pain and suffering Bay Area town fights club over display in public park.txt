'Pain and suffering': Bay Area town fights club over display in public park
-----
A towering steel cross that once sat on Albany Hill, the eucalyptus-laden mound off Interstate 80 that's adorned with high-rise condos looking out over the bay, is at the center of a legal battle surrounding First Amendment rights and religious expression on public land.The 28-foot-tall cross belongs to the Albany Lions Club, which erected the structure in 1971 on a section of Albany Hill that was owned at the time by Lions Club members Hubert and Ruth Call.
