S.F. luxury high-rise rebrands after flooding crisis. Can it woo new residents?
-----
New signage on the facade of a luxury high-rise in San Francisco's SoMa neighborhood offers a not-so-subtle clue that the owner of 33 Tehama St. is seeking to turn the page on the flooding crisis that displaced hundreds of tenants two years ago.Bold, white letters pasted across windows near the base of the 35-story tower that once read "33Tehama.com" have been replaced with "SperaSF.com." The words "Now Leasing" have been added.
