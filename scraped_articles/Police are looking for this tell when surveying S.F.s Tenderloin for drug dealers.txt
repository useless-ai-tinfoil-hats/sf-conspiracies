Police are looking for this ‘tell’ when surveying S.F.’s Tenderloin for drug dealers
-----
Law enforcement officials have repeatedly cited the same behavior in identifying suspects they later arrest on suspicion of drug dealing in San Francisco's Tenderloin neighborhood: a person constantly scanning the streets, as though on high alert for potential threats.A Chronicle review of 24 federal court affidavits for recent drug arrests in San Francisco and Oakland showed that in at least nine cases, officers observed people whose eyes darted over their surroundings.
