Exclusive: LGBTQ activist Cleve Jones to introduce new community fund, celebrate milestone birthday
-----
Cleve Jones did not expect to live to be 40.The legendary LGBTQ activist and protege of Harvey Milk announced his HIV positive status in a "60 Minutes" interview in 1985, a time when treatment for the disease was limited and often difficult to access. By the early 1990s, Jones was close to death but was saved by the trials of new drug combinations that came to be known as the AIDS cocktail.
Now, decades after his diagnosis, he is about to celebrate a milestone birthday.
