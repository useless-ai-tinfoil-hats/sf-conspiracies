America still doesn’t know how to talk about the incarceration of Japanese Americans
-----
Out the front windows of our bus, we could see acres of sun-dried grasses in hot and arid Northern California. On either side of the road: barbed-wire fences, like the ones many of our family members spent years behind, surrounded by armed guards and guard towers, living in crowded tar paper barracks with little to no privacy."How many of you have been here before, or were here during World War II?" our tour guide asked.
