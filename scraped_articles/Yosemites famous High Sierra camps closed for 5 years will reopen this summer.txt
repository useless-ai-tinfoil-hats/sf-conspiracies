Yosemite's historic High Sierra Camps, which have served as a springboard into the national park's backcountry for generations of Bay Areans, are slated to reopen this summer after being closed the past five years.Established a century ago and supplied by pack mules, the five rustic camps are set about 8 miles apart and form a large hiking loop through the granite wilderness east of Yosemite Valley and into Tuolumne Meadows.
