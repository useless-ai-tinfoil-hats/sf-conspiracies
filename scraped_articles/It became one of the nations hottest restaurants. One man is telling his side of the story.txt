It became one of the nation’s hottest restaurants. One man is telling his side of the story

For 2½ years, Richard Lupio often stood watch, alone, at the overnight fires burning in the fleet of hulking smokers outside Horn Barbecue in Oakland. He would get the brisket on the grates while listening to Mozart. He'd note the time and temperatures in a black Moleskine notebook tucked into his apron.
