Deep in the lab with California's last museum taxidermy specialist
-----
It is just after 5 a.m., and the Natural History Museum of Los Angeles is dark and silent, with just a few small spotlights illuminating strange shapes within the alcoves and dioramas of the North American and African Mammal halls."I like to be here when no one else is around, so I can see if anything has happened overnight," says Tim Bovard. "I look for damage, or pests, or maybe something needs cleaning, or eyes polished."
At 70, Bovard has become as rare as some of the animals
