Paris' $1.5 billion effort to clean up Seine for Olympics swim events at risk
-----
PARIS — Less than a tenth of an inch of rain fell in Paris Tuesday morning, which normally wouldn't be cause for alarm.But after the city spent a reported $1.5 billion to clean up the River Seine — which regularly has sewage flow into it during heavy rains thanks to Paris' archaic sewer system — it was cause for some alarm ahead of Friday's kickoff to the 2024 Olympics. The competition is supposed to host multiple swim events in the river, as well as a country-by-country parade of boats
