Months after Pride flag ban, a conservative school board loses its progressive superintendent
-----
The superintendent of a one-school Alameda County district that's been in turmoil since a controversial decision to ban the Pride flag last year will retire in June, a decision attributed largely to the chaos.Molleen Barnes has served as superintendent and principal of Sunol Glen School in rural Alameda County for 16 years. She announced her retirement in a letter to the school community on Wednesday that was published in Inform Sunol, an online newsletter.
