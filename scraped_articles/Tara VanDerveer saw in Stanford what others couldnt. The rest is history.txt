Tara VanDerveer saw in Stanford what others couldn’t. The rest is history
-----
When Tara VanDerveer left Ohio State to take the women's basketball head coaching job at Stanford, her father told her she would be unemployed after two months.He understood the risk associated with the move. VanDerveer, a two-time Big Ten Coach of the Year, had just led Ohio State to a 28-3 record and a trip to the 1985 Elite Eight. Meanwhile, Stanford had totaled just 14 wins over the past two seasons. Among the Cardinal's lowlights: a 32-point smackdown by … VanDerveer's Buckeyes.
