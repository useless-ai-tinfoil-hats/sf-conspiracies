Oakland would cut police, fire services if critical $105 million Coliseum deal falls through
-----
The city of Oakland has revealed contingency plans in case a critical $105 million deal to sell its share of the Coliseum to private developers falls through.The plan includes cutting an additional $63 million — the amount the city is banking on from the Coliseum sale to help it close the deficit for the next fiscal year — through reductions to city programs and staff in order to cover expenditures.
