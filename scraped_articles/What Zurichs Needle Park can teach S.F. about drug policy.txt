What Zurich’s ‘Needle Park’ can teach S.F. about drug policy
-----
The park was filthy. Trash lined the sidewalks. People huddled in groups injecting drugs or slept wrapped in blankets. Used syringes were everywhere, and paramedics were regularly called to help those overdosing.No, I'm not describing the Tenderloin, but Platzspitz Park in Zurich, where symptoms of drug use in the 1980s and '90s resembled San Francisco of today.
