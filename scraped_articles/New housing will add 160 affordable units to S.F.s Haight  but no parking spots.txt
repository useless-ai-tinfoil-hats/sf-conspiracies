New housing will add 160 affordable units to S.F.’s Haight — but no parking spots
-----
For the past six months, Amoeba Music co-owner Joe Goldmark has watched as an apartment building at 730 Stanyan rises next to his San Francisco store. The concrete of the third story has been poured and pretty soon it will be looming over the old "bowling" marquee left over from when the record shop was Park Bowl.Goldmark counts himself as an enthusiastic supporter of the 160-unit project's mission to bring low-cost housing to the corner of Haight and Stanyan, the former McDonald's site
