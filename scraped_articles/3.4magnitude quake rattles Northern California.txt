3.4-magnitude quake rattles Northern California
-----
A 3.4-magnitude earthquake was reported in Sonoma County on Monday morning, according to data from the U.S. Geological Survey.* Earthquake tracker: Mapping the latest quakes in California and across the world
The quake was reported at 8:45 a.m. about 1.2 miles east-southeast of The Geysers, a large geothermal field along the Sonoma and Lake County border in the Mayacama Mountains where earthquakes happen on a near-daily basis.
Reports of damage were not immediately available.
