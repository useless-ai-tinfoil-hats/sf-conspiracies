EPA fines Bay Area cities for failing to prevent sewage from entering the SF Bay
-----
The Environmental Protection Agency and the local San Francisco Bay Regional Water Quality Control Board have joined forces to fine East Bay cities and utility districts a total of $372,876 for failures to meet an agreement aimed at keeping untreated sewage out of the San Francisco Bay, according to a Thursday news release.The fines follow a deal that the EPA made in 2014 with the East Bay Municipal Utility District and the seven communities it serves to upgrade 1,600 miles of sewer
