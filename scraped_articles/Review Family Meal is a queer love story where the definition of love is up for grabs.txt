Review: ‘Family Meal’ is a queer love story where the definition of love is up for grabs
-----
When you pick up a Bryan Washington novel, it's best to know what you're getting into.What you can expect right off the bat: graphic sex with acquaintances, partners and strangers (and then more of that); mouthwatering descriptions of amazing culinary concoctions on nearly every page; and a pretty on-point depiction of a neighborhood that's deep in the throes of gentrification without its longtime inhabitants' consent.
