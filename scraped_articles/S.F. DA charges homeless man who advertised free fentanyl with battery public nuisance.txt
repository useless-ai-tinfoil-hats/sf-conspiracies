S.F. DA charges homeless man who advertised ‘free fentanyl’ with battery, public nuisance
-----
The homeless man who had been advertising free fentanyl across from a San Francisco private school has been charged with battery and creating a public nuisance, according to District Attorney Brooke Jenkins.Joseph Adam Moore sparked the ire of Richmond District residents when he set up a temporary dwelling on Ninth Avenue across from the Stella Maris Academy Catholic school and hung up signs reading "meth for stolen items" and "free fentanyl 4 new users."
