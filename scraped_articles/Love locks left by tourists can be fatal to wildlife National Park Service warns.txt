‘Love locks’ left by tourists can be fatal to wildlife, National Park Service warns
-----
The National Park Service is urging visitors to refrain from leaving "love locks" — personally inscribed padlocks attached to fencing by couples as a symbol of their everlasting devotion — around its tourist areas due to the threat they pose to wildlife.Park rangers have been removing such padlocks left behind at popular overlooks at the Grand Canyon, denouncing the act as "littering" and a type of "graffiti."
