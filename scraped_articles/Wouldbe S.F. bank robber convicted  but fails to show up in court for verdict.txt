Would-be S.F. bank robber convicted — but fails to show up in court for verdict
-----
A San Francisco jury on Tuesday convicted a man of attempted second-degree robbery for trying to rob a bank near Japantown — a crime foiled after a bank teller pressed a silent alarm button to summon police.Franklin Fardella, 39, faces up to three years in prison for the caper on June 26, when he entered a bank on the 1300 block of Post Street and handed a note to a teller, demanding money.
