New BART fare gates are meant to reduce ‘mayhem.’ Here’s how and when they’ll be installed
-----
BART riders have long been accustomed to seeing people casually jump over, crawl under, or force their way through the rail system's orange, fin-like gates without paying a fare.That might end soon.
Thursday, riders at BART's West Oakland Station are expected to be the first to experience the new way of entering the rail system — through fare gates more than 7 feet tall, with sturdy and transparent polycarbonate doors.
