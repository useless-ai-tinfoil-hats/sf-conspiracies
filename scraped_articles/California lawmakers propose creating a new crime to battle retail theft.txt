California lawmakers propose creating a new crime to battle retail theft
-----
Lawmakers in the California state Assembly on Thursday unveiled a sweeping package of measures aimed at curbing organized retail theft, including a provision to make the intent to sell stolen property a new crime punishable by up to three years in prison.Known as the California Retail Theft Reduction Act, the package was introduced by three Democratic members of the state Assembly: Speaker Robert Rivas of Salinas, Rick Chavez Zbur of Los Angeles, and Kevin McCarty of Sacramento.
