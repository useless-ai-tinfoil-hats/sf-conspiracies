Scientists 'saddened' by near-total loss of endangered species in Gulf of California
-----
Large schools of unusual sharks that once swarmed two underwater mountains dotting the southern Gulf of California are disappearing, and overfishing may be the culprit, according to a new study slated to be published in the journal Marine Policy next month.Scalloped hammerhead sharks, known for their distinctive flattened faces and widely spaced eyes, are a semi-pelagic species, meaning they spend part of their life at the bottom of the ocean where they hunt for prey buried in the sea
