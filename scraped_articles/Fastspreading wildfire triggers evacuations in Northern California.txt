'Fast-spreading' wildfire triggers evacuations in Northern California
-----
A wildfire sparked in Northern California's Butte County on Monday evening rapidly grew to over 600 acres and triggered a series of evacuations.The Apache Fire is burning south of Oroville near Apache Hill Road and Crestmont Avenue near the community of Palermo, according to Cal Fire. The blaze ignited at 7:21 p.m. Two structures have been destroyed and one person injured, the agency said.
