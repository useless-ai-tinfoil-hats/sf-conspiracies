KQED to lay off 18 to 25 employees after buyout packages weren't enough to offset costs
-----
KQED, the Bay Area's most notable public broadcaster, is expected to lay off between 18 and 25 employees after recently initiating staff buyout packages to help with a budget deficit, SFGATE has learned.In a Monday email to KQED staff viewed by SFGATE, CEO Michael Isip said a small number of employees took the early retirement package, but it won't be enough to offset the financial gap.
