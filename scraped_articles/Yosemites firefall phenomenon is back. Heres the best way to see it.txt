Yosemite’s ‘firefall’ phenomenon is back. Here’s the best way to see it
-----
The time is almost ripe for the magical, ephemeral and highly photogenic "firefall" phenomenon that draws crowds of spectators to Yosemite Valley each chilly February.Firefall has come to refer to the special cocktail of late-day, mid-winter sunlight that sometimes transforms the misting snowmelt stream at Horsetail Fall into a dramatic show of fiery-orange liquid pouring thousands of feet down El Capitan's ice-cold granite.
