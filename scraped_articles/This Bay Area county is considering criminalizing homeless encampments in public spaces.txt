This Bay Area county is considering criminalizing homeless encampments in public spaces
-----
The San Mateo County Board of Supervisors will consider a proposal Tuesday that would make it a misdemeanor to live in an encampment in public spaces in unincorporated areas when shelter beds are available.Officials would move forward with criminal charges if someone turned down offers of shelter beds twice.
County staff and elected officials said their goal is to make homelessness brief and rare. They cited the public safety and health hazards of encampments.
