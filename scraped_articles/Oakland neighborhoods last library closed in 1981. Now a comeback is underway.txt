Oakland neighborhood’s last library closed in 1981. Now a comeback is underway
-----
Residents of the Hoover-Foster area of West Oakland had access to reading rooms and libraries for more than a century, until the facilities were shuttered as a result of decades of redlining and disinvestment in Black neighborhoods in the mid-20th century.Today the nearest branch is more than a mile away — a "significant limitation" for a neighborhood bordered entirely by major highways and roads, according to the grassroots nonprofit Friends of Hoover Durant Public Library.
