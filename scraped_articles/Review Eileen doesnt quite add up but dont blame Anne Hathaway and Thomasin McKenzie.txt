Review: ‘Eileen’ doesn’t quite add up, but don’t blame Anne Hathaway and Thomasin McKenzie
-----
"Eileen" should be studied in film schools as an example of what smart direction and good acting can — and can't — do with a problematic script.Good acting and direction can create a mood, a feeling of tension and ominousness, as well as a sense that something big is just about to happen. They can establish a connection between the characters that hints in interesting directions and makes us wonder where things are going.
