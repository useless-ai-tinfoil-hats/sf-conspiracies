Review: Berkeley Rep’s ‘The Far Country,’ set on Angel Island, probes the promise and lie of immigration
-----
Over and over, the Chinese endure all manner of indignity from nameless white American men in uniform. The interrogators attack a mother's virtue. They ask repetitive, Kafkaesque questions about matters of such trivial consequence that words both become poetic and void themselves of meaning. And the Chinese, hoping for citizenship papers or the right to leave and reenter the U.S. in the era of the Chinese Exclusion Act, can't protest or even look upset.
