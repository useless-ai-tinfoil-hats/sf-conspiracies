Bay Area pilot crash-lands upside down on NorCal field
-----
A Bay Area pilot landed his single-engine aircraft upside down in a wildlife refuge about 250 miles north of San Francisco Thursday morning, officials said.Humboldt County sheriff's deputies were dispatched to respond to the crash around 10:35 a.m. Officials located the fixed winged aircraft belly up in a dry marsh field within the Humboldt Bay National Wildlife Refuge near Loleta.
The aircraft's nose tore into the wet field to create a massive divot in the earth.
