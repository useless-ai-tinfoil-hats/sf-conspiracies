Review: Immersive show about Fillmore’s redevelopment scheme asks which side you’re on
-----
The cymbals sound like a match perpetually being struck. The bass solo envelops each note in the scale at once. On the guitar, Kelyn Crapp anticipates the driving rhythm of Chuck Berry.If you close your eyes for a moment at "The Fillmore Eclipse," you can fool yourself into thinking you're really there: at a jazz club in the Fillmore District in the 1950s, on the very night before it's about to be bulldozed as part of the neighborhood's notorious redevelopment scheme.
