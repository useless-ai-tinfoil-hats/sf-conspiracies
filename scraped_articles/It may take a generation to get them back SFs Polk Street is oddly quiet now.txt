'It may take a generation to get them back': SF's Polk Street is oddly quiet now
-----
For years, a familiar weekend ritual unfolded across the Bay Area. Every Friday and Saturday night, young people from Walnut Creek and Lafayette sprayed on cologne and threw on their heels before riding BART to Civic Center. From there, they headed north, like summering birds, to buildings with mysterious names, like "The Hemlock" and "McTeague's." They'd ping-pong between bars, like bees zipping from flower to flower.
