Officials clear out ‘elaborate’ California caves unhoused people used for shelter
-----
Unhoused people in California's Central Valley dug caves in a riverbank to live in, officials said, until local authorities and volunteers cleared the caverns over the weekend, citing safety concerns.People who had been living in the eight caves along the Tuolumne River in the Modesto area had fashioned them into "elaborate" makeshift homes, CBS Sacramento reported.
