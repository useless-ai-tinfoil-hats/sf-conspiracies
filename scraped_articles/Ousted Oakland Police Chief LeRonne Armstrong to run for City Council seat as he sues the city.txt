Ousted Oakland Police Chief LeRonne Armstrong to run for City Council seat as he sues the city
-----
An ousted police chief who filed a scorching lawsuit against Oakland and its mayor is now running for citywide office, pledging to clamp down on crime, close a budget deficit and clean up "embarrassing" street conditions.LeRonne Armstrong, 51, said in an interview that he was wronged by city leaders but has not given up on its residents. He filed paperwork Wednesday morning to enter a crowded race for the City Council's at-large seat.
