Eighty will be charged for shutting down Bay Bridge in protest calling for Gaza cease fire
-----
Eighty people will be charged with unlawful public assembly, among other misdemeanors, for a November protest that shut down traffic across the Bay Bridge for hours, the San Francisco District Attorney's Office announced Friday.On the morning of Nov. 16, protesters shut down the westbound lanes of the Bay Bridge, calling for a cease fire in Gaza while world leaders met in San Francisco for the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation summit.
