SFMOMA faces layoffs as foot traffic stagnates
-----
The San Francisco Museum of Modern Art has cut 20 staff positions, citing financial strain from stagnant foot traffic.In a letter published on Friday, SFMOMA director Chris Bedford announced that the museum would lay off seven employees and close thirteen open but unfilled positions. Bedford's letter listed the layoffs as one of a handful of "strategic decisions" the museum was taking to remain financially sustainable.
