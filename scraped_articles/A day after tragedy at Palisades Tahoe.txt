A day after tragedy at Palisades Tahoe
-----
It's 7:55 a.m., and the parking lot at Palisades Tahoe is empty. I parked in the first row of a lot that's often nearly full by early morning on powder days. Normally, this would be one of the most exuberant days of the winter at Palisades Tahoe: the first real powder day of 2024, following a storm that deposited more than a foot of snow on Lake Tahoe in the last 24 hours.But the mood is far from jovial the morning after an in-bounds avalanche killed a 66-year-old skier and injured three
