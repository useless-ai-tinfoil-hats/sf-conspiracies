S.F.’s teen lion dancers are international champions chasing a dream — crossover success
-----
In a San Francisco warehouse on a late stormy night, Makeila Zhou, 16, and Winson Deng, 19, leapt across a series of metal discs held aloft by 6-foot-tall metal poles called the jong. The two had executed the maneuver, called a fly, before — but this time they wore an oversize lion costume.Zhou was the lion's head, obscured under a fur-lined mask made of bamboo and papier-mâché, while Deng simulated the lion's body draped in a scalloped pink cape.
