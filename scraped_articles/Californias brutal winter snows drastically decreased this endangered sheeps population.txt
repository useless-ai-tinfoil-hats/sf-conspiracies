California's brutal winter snows drastically decreased this endangered sheep's population
-----
The population of endangered bighorn sheep in the Sierra Nevada plunged drastically due to last winter's deep snows.And with another wet winter potentially on the horizon, scientists are concerned for the animals' future.
About 550 Sierra Nevada bighorn sheep were living in the High Sierra going into the winter, but over the course of season that dropped to about 360, according to Tom Stephenson, program leader for the Department of Fish and Wildlife's Sierra Nevada Bighorn Sheep
