Utah national park landmark, millions of years old, finally collapses
-----
An iconic landmark that stood when dinosaurs roamed the Earth and sees more than 2 million visitors per year collapsed Thursday, park officials said.Double Arch, a rock formation looming over Lake Powell in Utah's Glen Canyon National Recreation Area, is made out of 190-million-year-old Navajo Sandstone and has been subject to weather-related erosion over time.
