What to know about the partial solar eclipse in California
-----
LATEST, April 8, 10:35 a.m.: The partial solar eclipse was underway in California just after 10 a.m. Monday. The eclipse started in San Francisco at 10:14 a.m., with the moon first starting to cross the face of the sun. At 10:30 a.m., the sun looked like a small bite had been taken out of it.The partial solar eclipse will reach maximum in the SF Bay Area at about 11:13 a.m. and will end at about 12:15 p.m. Anyone looking at the partial eclipse, needs to wear special eclipse glasses.
