One of California's great search missions led to a lonely mountain grave
-----
South of Yosemite, miles from anything resembling civilization, lie the Minarets. Twelve thousand feet high, their granite teeth tear jagged bites across the horizon. They are arresting, remote and unforgiving.Each time the wind cuts through the peaks, it sweeps over a lonely tomb. For more than a century, the remains of a young Californian have rested among the Minarets.
