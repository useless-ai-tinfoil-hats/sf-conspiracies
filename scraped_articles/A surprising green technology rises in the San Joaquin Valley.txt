A surprising green technology rises in the San Joaquin Valley
-----
TRACY — A first-of-its-kind California startup has found a way to take carbon dioxide out of their air and store it in concrete — and it is putting the technology to work in Tracy.As air blows through Heirloom Carbon Technologies' facility, hundreds of trays of calcium oxide rock lie in wait to absorb carbon dioxide in the air.
The key ingredient to Heirloom's CO2 direct air capture is limestone, which is made up of CO2 and calcium oxide.
