Vintage 1920s building in downtown S.F. facing foreclosure
-----
Fifteen years ago, a Marin County real estate investor swooped into downtown San Francisco to buy the Chancery Building, a seven-story vintage, 1920s property on Market Street that became available after its previous owner declared bankruptcy. At the time, the building was described as "recession ready." This week, it appears to be in distress with its lender.A representative of Chelsea Pacific Holdings LLC, a Tiburon real estate investment firm, declined to provide comment on a notice
