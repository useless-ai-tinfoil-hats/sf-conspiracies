The forgotten Calif. man hired to kill Fidel Castro

In the early 1950s, Frank Sinatra found himself at loose ends. The public's musical tastes had shifted and so had his voice. Headlines about his divorce (and his affair with actress Ava Gardner, whom he later married) dogged him in the press. His career slumped. Then, Sinatra got wind of a picture, "From Here to Eternity," and saw an opportunity for a comeback.
