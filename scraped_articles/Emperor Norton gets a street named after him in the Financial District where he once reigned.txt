Emperor Norton gets a street named after him in the Financial District, where he once reigned
-----
Dressed in his Civil War uniform and top hat festooned with feathers, Emperor Joshua Abraham Norton came out of his boarding house, Eureka Lodging on Commercial Street, and aided by his walking stick, made his way to the corner of Montgomery Street.Something did not look quite right, and he was quick to detect that the street sign read "Emperor Norton Pl." Just then a marching band struck up "California Here I Come," and he realized what the fuss was about. Sunday was the 164th
