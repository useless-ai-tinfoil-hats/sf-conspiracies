‘A true legend’: SJSU judo coach who trained 22 Olympians dies at 104
-----
Renowned San Jose State University judo coach Yoshihiro "Yosh" Uchida has died at the age of 104, the university announced Friday.Born in Calexico (Imperial County) in 1920, Uchida became SJSU's judo coach in 1940 when he enrolled at the university as a student, officials said. After serving four years in the army during World War II, Uchida returned to the school as its judo coach. He graduated from SJSU with his bachelor's degree in biological sciences in 1947, the university said.
