S.F. mansion once owned by Nicolas Cage sells after a month on market
-----
Actor Nicolas Cage's former San Francisco party palace, a five-bedroom Victorian mansion in Pacific Heights, has changed hands after just a month on the market.Listed on Feb. 16 for $3.5 million, the property at 1945 Franklin St. sold on March 19 for $3.3 million, according to real estate listing websites.
In addition to Cage, who threw wild parties there, the grand house "has had its share of illustrious owners and visitors," said its listing agent, Helena Zaludova of Compass.
