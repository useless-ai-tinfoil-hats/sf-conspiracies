This tiny new Bay Area private school aims to radically depart from ‘dropout factories’
-----
The building behind a church and next to a liquor store in East Oakland doesn't look like a typical school. And the small, free private school opening this month doesn't plan to operate like one either. That's because traditional public schools have largely failed the students Freedom School plans to serve."Most of public education is well-organized dropout factories," said César A. Cruz, the school's principal.
