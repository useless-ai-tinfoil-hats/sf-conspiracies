Until 2003, my life was happily uneventful — I was in good health, had a supportive family and partner, and my career was blossoming. I loved my life in the Bay Area — I was biking, hiking, learning to dance and going to museums, concerts and the theater. Then, one Friday morning in September, I awoke to a large dark smudge in the vision of my left eye; within six weeks, I was diagnosed with multiple sclerosis.I remember crying on the Richmond BART line on my way home from the doctor
