Wildfire in California national park grows nearly 25%, triggers evacuations
-----
A wildfire ignited by lightning on Aug. 3 in the southwest corner of Sequoia National Park has been quiet for the past week but exploded Tuesday into Wednesday amid dry, hot weather, growing nearly 25% in size and triggering evacuations, officials said.The Coffee Pot Fire that started southeast of the town of Three Rivers in Tulare County grew from 3,678 acres on Tuesday to 4,552 acres on Wednesday and has no containment, according to park officials.
