3.9 earthquake strikes Humboldt County
-----
An earthquake of magnitude 3.9 struck about 2 miles south of Petrolia (Humboldt County) at 8:41 a.m. Sunday, according to the U.S. Geological Survey.The quake struck in the area around Eureka where four temblors, ranging in magnitude from 3.7 to 4.9, were detected Saturday morning.
Petrolia, a frequent site of earthquakes, is near the Mendocino Triple Junction, a seismically active area where three fault lines meet.
Reports of damage were not immediately available.
