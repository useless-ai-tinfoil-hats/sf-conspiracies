‘Pure fear’: Violent crime in Oakland rose 21% last year. Residents worry it will define the city
-----
Oakland police Sgt. Sean Hall addressed the quiet, watchful crowd gathered at a church near Lake Merritt on a recent Wednesday evening. Eyes sweeping the audience, Hall flashed an unassuming smile.He was there to outline the department's new patrol strategy to Grand Lake Neighbors, a group that meets monthly to discuss everything from street safety to parking enforcement. The sergeant's mission: present a friendly face and try to instill optimism at a moment when crime is surging.
