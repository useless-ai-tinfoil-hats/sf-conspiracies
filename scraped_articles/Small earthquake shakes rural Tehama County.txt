Small earthquake shakes rural Tehama County
-----
A 3.1 magnitude earthquake rattled a woodsy, unincorporated community in far Northern California on Tuesday evening, the United States Geological Survey said.The quake erupted in the Mendocino National Forest, 14.9 miles west of Rancho Tehama Reserve in Tehama County, shortly after 7 p.m., according to the USGS. Officials with the USGS said the quake produced weak shaking.
There were no immediate reports of injuries or damage to structures.
