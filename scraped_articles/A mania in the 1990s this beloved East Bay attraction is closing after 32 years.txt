A 'mania' in the 1990s, this beloved East Bay attraction is closing after 32 years
-----
When the Q-ZAR laser tag craze first zapped its way across the East Bay in the '90s, the Oakland Tribune described the team-based shooting game as "an addiction" that was so exciting it sent some players into a "mania." Over 30 years later, the fad is winding down as one of the last locations standing prepares to close for good.Last week, Q-ZAR Laser Tag in Concord announced its plans to shut down its outpost on the corner of Willow Pass Road and Colfax Street.
