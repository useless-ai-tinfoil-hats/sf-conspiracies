Review: Corrosive history of Ukraine and Soviet Union is seen through the eyes of a soldier
-----
Why would the main character of Sasha Vasilyuk's sharply observed first novel — an elderly Ukrainian veteran named Yefim Shulman, who was wounded in a long-ago World War II battle — set fire to his military ID and other items from his Soviet Red Army days?The answer tells us a lot about the protagonist and his country. "In life," Vasilyuk writes, "especially in a Ukrainian life, one never knew what would come next."
"Your Presence Is Mandatory" is a gratifyingly specific portrait
