This man ran every Bay to Breakers since 1975 — through rain, pain and knee replacement
-----
Pete Shaw was a new San Francisco resident in 1974, working on the fifth floor of 180 New Montgomery St. at Howard, when a river of humanity began flowing by on the street below.It was a relative trickle back then, but the offbeat Bay to Breakers vibes were already strong.
"I looked out the window and thought, 'What are these guys doing, and where did they come from? ' " Shaw said. "It just looked like so much fun."
So Shaw ran the next year — and has run it every year since,
