S.F. office vacancy hits staggering new record, with 30 million square feet empty
-----
San Francisco's office market hit another grim milestone in the third quarter, with a record-high 33.9% vacancy rate — or nearly 30 million square feet listed for lease or sublease — according to preliminary data from real estate brokerage CBRE.Despite some growth from artificial intelligence companies, a net 1.9 million square feet of offices became vacant, pushing the vacancy rate up 2.3 percentage points from the second quarter. Roughly 150,000 workers could fit in all the empty space
