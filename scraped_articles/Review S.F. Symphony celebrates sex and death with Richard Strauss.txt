Review: S.F. Symphony celebrates sex and death with Richard Strauss
-----
The orchestral tone poems that Richard Strauss wrote during his early maturity, before turning his attention to opera, are odd and wondrous creations. Some, like "Don Quixote," are literary, using narrative as a structural device. Others are philosophical or pictorial or both.For her latest visit with the San Francisco Symphony, guest conductor Karina Canellakis led the orchestra in a matched pair of these pieces, giving the audience a full helping of the composer's instrumental mastery
