Homicides in the biggest Bay Area cities declined for the first time in four years in 2023
-----
After seeing homicides rise for four straight years amid the upheaval of the pandemic, the Bay Area's biggest cities together recorded a 4% decrease in 2023, a welcome reversal that police officials and community leaders hope will continue.Killings across the region's 15 most populous cities dropped from a total of 305 in 2022 to 292 last year, according to a Chronicle analysis of police data.
