Isolation. Trauma. Returning to the closet. State’s aging LGBTQ population faces unique challenges
-----
Infected with HIV in San Francisco in the 1980s, Arturo Jackson says he can count 100 people — friends, acquaintances, colleagues, lovers — who died in their 20s of AIDS. He always thought he'd be one of them.Now 63 and living in Sacramento, Jackson instead is facing a whole new era of challenges as a member of California's aging LGBTQ community.
Last year, Jackson lost his partner of more than 40 years, Weston Shirley, and he struggled to find mental health resources to process
