Traditional Polynesian canoe, loaded with goodwill, sails into S.F. Bay
-----
After 2,830 miles navigating by stars, waves and birds, and propelled by wind, the Polynesian voyaging canoe Hokulea was scheduled to sail into Aquatic Park Cove at noon Sunday. It was 18 minutes late, which did not matter to hundreds of people waiting in the grandstand.When the canoe rounded the breakwater, accompanied by paddlers on outriggers and boards, the crowd went silent in a reverie interrupted only by the ancient greeting of conch shells.
