‘Family Reservations’ uses a Bay Area dining icon as a symbol of family trauma
-----
In 2024, the long-accepted idea of a cult chef personality has been toppled, first by the headlines, then by fictional works such as the horror satire movie "The Menu" and comedy-drama series "The Bear." Joining those properties is Liza Palmer's latest novel, "Family Reservations," which spins a tale of a toxic Bay Area restaurant dynasty inspired by some very real culinary icons.Set in Marin County and San Francisco, Palmer's book follows a trailblazing 1970s chef and restaurant owner
