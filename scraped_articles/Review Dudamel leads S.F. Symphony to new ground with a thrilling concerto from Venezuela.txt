Review: Dudamel leads S.F. Symphony to new ground with a thrilling concerto from Venezuela
-----
Orchestra concerts have many possible virtues, from dramatic intensity to sheer expressive beauty. Novelty — the chance to hear something unlike anything you've heard before — rarely ranks high on the list.That's one thing that made the San Francisco Symphony's concert on Friday, Nov. 24, so memorable. Guest conductor Gustavo Dudamel led the orchestra's first performance of "Odisea" ("Odyssey"), a new concerto by Venezuelan composer Gonzalo Grau for the guitar-like cuatro.
