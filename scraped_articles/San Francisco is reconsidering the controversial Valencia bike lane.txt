San Francisco is reconsidering the controversial Valencia bike lane
-----
Change may be on the horizon for one of San Francisco's most contentious public corridors. San Francisco Municipal Transportation Agency is considering scrapping Valencia Street's controversial center bike lane after its 12 month pilot period ends.The center bike lane, which runs between two lanes of car traffic, was designed to protect cyclists on Valencia Street from dooring and double-parked cars. Valencia had one of the highest collision rates in the city, according to the SFMTA.
