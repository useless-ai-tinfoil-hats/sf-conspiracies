After big fight, S.F. Mission District tiny home village for homeless will move forward
-----
A delayed plan to build a tiny home village in the Mission is expected to finally break ground this winter after a yearslong fight, Supervisor Hillary Ronen told the Chronicle.The San Francisco Department of Homelessness and Supportive Housing is getting ready to open 60 cabins serving up to 68 guests at 1979 Mission St., a vacant lot where affordable housing is slated to break ground in about two years.
