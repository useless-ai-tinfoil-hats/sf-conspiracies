Officials working to clean up oil patch spotted in the ocean near Huntington Beach
-----
The U.S. Coast Guard and Southern California officials began work Friday to clean an oil patch that was spotted Thursday night in the Pacific Ocean, a few miles off the coast of Huntington Beach in Orange County.U.S. Coast Guard Petty Officer First Class Richard Brahm told SFGATE that officials received a report of a sheen — a very thin layer of oil that floats on the ocean's surface — at 7:30 p.m. Thursday.
