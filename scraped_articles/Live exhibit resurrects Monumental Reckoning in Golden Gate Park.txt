Live exhibit resurrects ‘Monumental Reckoning’ in Golden Gate Park
-----
The south end of the Music Concourse in San Francisco's Golden Gate Park, flanking an open plaza with fountains and gridded trees, has served as a battleground for the country's racial reckonings.A statue of Francis Scott Key, a slaveholder who wrote "The Star-Spangled Banner" with a stanza understood to celebrate the deaths of slaves, stood in the park for more than a century. In 2020, protesters tore it down in the aftermath of the murder of George Floyd.
