TravelSkills 12-20-23 Bay Area locals flock to this historic California Gold Rush town
-----
A Sierra Nevada mountain town was at a crossroads after the I-80 freeway was built. Cities across California were facelifting their downtowns to fit a new urban trend but Nevada City decided to double down on its Gold Rush heritage.A project in 1972 pushed for preservation; overhead wires were removed downtown to make way for gas lamps while bright neon signs on local businesses were placed with simpler signage. "It just made the difference night and day," the local museum director said.
