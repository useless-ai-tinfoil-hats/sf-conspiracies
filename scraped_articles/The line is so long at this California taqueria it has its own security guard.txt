The line is so long at this California taqueria, it has its own security guard
-----
At 10:55 a.m. on a Wednesday morning, there are already close to 20 people on the sidewalk in front of Tito's Tacos, the Los Angeles sunshine beating down on the pavement. As cars whiz by on Washington Place in the shadow of the 405 freeway, customers wait patiently for the taco stand to flip the "Window Open" signs at the ordering counter.It's the calm before the storm of a typical day at one of LA's busiest and most beloved (and debated) taquerias, where the line will often stretch
