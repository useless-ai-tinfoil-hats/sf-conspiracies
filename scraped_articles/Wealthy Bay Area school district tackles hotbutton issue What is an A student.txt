Wealthy Bay Area school district tackles hot-button issue: What is an ‘A’ student?
-----
A few years ago, officials at Palo Alto Unified noticed that one district high school was giving out far fewer D's and F's than previously, instead giving students a "no mark," which allowed additional time to complete assignments or take exams.But teachers there weren't giving all students that second chance for a better grade. They were disproportionately giving it to white and Asian American students. Black and brown students were still getting D's and F's.
