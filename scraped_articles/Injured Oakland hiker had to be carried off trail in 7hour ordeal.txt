Injured Oakland hiker had to be carried off trail in 7-hour ordeal
-----
Rescuers spent more than seven hours carrying an Oakland man down a mountain after he was injured while hiking one of the hardest trails in New Hampshire.The Huntington Ravine Trail is the hardest trail in the White Mountain National Forest and requires hikers to rock climb and use ropes, according to New Hampshire Fish and Game Department. Officials said Izem Guendoud, a 31-year-old Oakland man, was attempting to hike the trail on Tuesday when he slipped and fell off the trail.
