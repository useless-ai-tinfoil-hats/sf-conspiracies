Review: Playwright August Wilson played to the hilt in one-man theater show
-----
August Wilson might not have dreamed how far his talent and drive would take him when he was a struggling poet kicking around Pittsburgh's Hill District in the 1960s, doing and promptly quitting menial jobs, getting into scrapes in bars and moving back in with his mother when he was broke.
