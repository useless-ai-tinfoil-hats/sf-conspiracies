How a mysterious bachelor gave San Francisco its famed aquarium
-----
From the beginning, nothing about Ignatz Steinhart screamed "aquarium."The banker and philanthropist was born in 1840 in Sulzbach, Bavaria, as landlocked of a town as you'll find in Europe. He had no children and no biology degrees, and in dozens of quotes in the San Francisco Chronicle he mentions no lifelong love for aquatic wildlife.
