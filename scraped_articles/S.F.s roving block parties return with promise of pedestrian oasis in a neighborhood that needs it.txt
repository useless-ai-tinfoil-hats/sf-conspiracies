S.F.’s roving block parties return with promise of ‘pedestrian oasis’ in a neighborhood that needs it
-----
For the first time in several years, Sunday Streets opened its season of roving San Francisco block parties in the Tenderloin, a risky start for Livable City, which stages the events. But its co-host, the St. Anthony Foundation, made it worth the risk by holding a soft opening of an 80-foot parklet on the corner of Golden Gate Avenue and Jones Street that was in use the minute the protective fencing was removed around it.Also on offer was the announcement of an $8 million federal grant
