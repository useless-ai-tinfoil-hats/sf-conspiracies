KQED eliminates San Jose office space in response to budget crisis
-----
KQED plans to shutter its downtown San Jose office, removing its brand signage from the 50 West office tower, as part of broader cost-cutting measures at one of the nation's most prominent public media organizations.Spokesperson Peter Cavagnaro told the Chronicle the move was necessary to confront the outlet's growing budget shortfall.
