Koda Farms, whose heirloom rice has been prized by chefs and home cooks alike for almost a century, is shutting down.The closure of the family-owned farm in Merced County, which Keisaburo Koda started 97 years ago, was first reported by the New York Times. His grandchildren, third-generation farmers Robin and Ross Koda, run the farm today. The rising costs of farming in California, from fertilizer to insurance and labor, have put undue pressure on the small operation.
