Nancy Seamons Crookston, Alameda painter ‘at the top of the art world,’ dies at 74
-----
Carrying her backpack of sketchbooks and pens, Nancy Seamons Crookston arrived every morning at 7 to Ole's Waffle Shop in Alameda to take her customary table marked by a gold-plated plaque with her name on it to sketch the chef and servers over her standard order of one poached egg on wheat toast, with mint tea.She'd then walk home to her studio to begin her day's work in oil figurative painting — a nearly two-decade routine that served her well.
