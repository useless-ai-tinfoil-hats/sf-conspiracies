Review: How the outdoors shaped the girls who grew up to be Harriet Tubman, Louisa May Alcott and others
-----
In the introduction to her new book, "Wild Girls," Harvard University Professor and 2011 MacArthur Fellowship recipient Tiya Miles tells a moving story about growing up near the Ohio River and a winter so cold that the river was blanketed in a sheet of ice 12 inches thick. She compares that experience to the six winters during the 1850s when the river froze and provided a rare ice bridge for enslaved Blacks to escape to freedom."This wily river was my inheritance as a Black single girl
