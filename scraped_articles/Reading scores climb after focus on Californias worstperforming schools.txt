Reading scores climb after focus on California’s worst-performing schools
-----
California's $53 million investment in teaching its youngest and lowest-performing students to read has shown dramatic — and relatively fast — results, researchers at Stanford have found.In a study released Sunday night, researchers at Stanford's Graduate School of Education found that the percentage of third graders in the program who met or nearly met the state reading standards rose 6 percentage points compared with students at similar schools.
