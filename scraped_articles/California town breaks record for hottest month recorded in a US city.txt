California town breaks record for hottest month recorded in a US city
-----
A small California town along historic Route 66 entered the record books last week, setting the new high bar for the hottest monthly average temperature recorded in a U.S. city.Needles recorded a preliminary average monthly temperature of 103.2 degrees in July, according to the Arizona State Climate Office. This surpassed the previous monthly temperature record of 102.7 degrees recorded in Phoenix last July. The temperature averages daytime highs and nighttime lows for the month.
