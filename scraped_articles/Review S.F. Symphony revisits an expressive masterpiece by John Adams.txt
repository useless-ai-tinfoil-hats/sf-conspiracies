Review: S.F. Symphony revisits an expressive masterpiece by John Adams
-----
"Naïve and Sentimental Music," John Adams' breathtakingly virtuosic 1998 tone poem, is one of the treasures of the orchestral repertoire, but we don't get a chance to hear it nearly enough.The performance given in Davies Symphony Hall Friday, Oct. 6, by the San Francisco Symphony and Music Director Esa-Pekka Salonen — a rendition full of instrumental power and tender clarity — was the piece's first local airing in nearly 20 years. It was like a reunion with an old friend.
