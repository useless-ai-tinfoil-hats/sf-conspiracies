Death penalty likely to remain on hold under California’s next governor
-----
After declaring California's first-ever moratorium on executions in 2019, Gov. Gavin Newsom is due to leave office in January 2027. But his shutdown of the state's death chamber is likely to continue after his departure.Three of the declared Democratic candidates for governor — state school Superintendent Tony Thurmond, former state Controller Betty Yee and state Sen. Toni Atkins — say they oppose the death penalty and will not approve any executions if elected. Lt. Gov. Eleni Kounalakis
