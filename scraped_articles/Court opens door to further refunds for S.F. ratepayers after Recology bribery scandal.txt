Court opens door to further refunds for S.F. ratepayers after Recology bribery scandal
-----
A state appeals court has opened the door to more potential refunds for San Francisco residents from their trash-collection company, Recology, in addition to the $94.5 million they have received for overpayments related to the bribery of former city Public Works Director Mohammed Nuru.Recology paid the earlier refunds to its 162,000 customers in San Francisco in a July 2021 settlement with the city, whose investigators found that the company had improperly raised rates for four years
