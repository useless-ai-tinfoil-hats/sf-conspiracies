'Super alien': New beach-dwelling species discovered in Bay Area
-----
Maddy Frey set out before sunrise to catch the low tide, scanning the darkened shoreline of Horseshoe Cove in Bodega Bay with a trowel in one hand and a bucket in the other.It was the summer of 2020, and the 20-year-old undergraduate researcher at UC Davis, now a scientific aide for the California Department of Fish and Wildlife, was looking for the wriggling, predatory species that lurked on the private beach just steps from the Bodega Marine Laboratory: ribbon worms.
