UC president’s Berkeley home was hit with racist graffiti and vandalism, but regents reject $13M solution
-----
The University of California regents are wrestling with how best to protect the system's leader, President Michael Drake, whose Berkeley home has been the target of vandals — including racist graffiti, smashed windows and references to the Jan. 6, 2021 insurrection — since UC bought it last year.What the regents won't do is buy Drake a nearly $13 million home in Piedmont. They rejected that option at a meeting last week, held behind closed doors because it dealt with matters of personnel
