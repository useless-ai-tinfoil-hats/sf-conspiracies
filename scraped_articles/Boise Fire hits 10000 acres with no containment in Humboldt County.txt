Boise Fire hits 10,000 acres with no containment in Humboldt County
-----
The Boise Fire burning in Humboldt County has ballooned to more than 10,000 acres after igniting Friday, making it the largest wildfire with zero containment currently burning in California.The fire is burning in Six Rivers National Forest, approximately 5 miles southwest of Orleans, an unincorporated community along state Route 96. Evacuations are in effect for parts of Humboldt and Siskiyou counties; the latest information can be found via Humboldt County's Office of Emergency Services
