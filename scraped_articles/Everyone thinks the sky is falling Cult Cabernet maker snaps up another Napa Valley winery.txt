‘Everyone thinks the sky is falling’: Cult Cabernet maker snaps up another Napa Valley winery
-----
The most prolific winery and vineyard buyer in Napa Valley of late isn't a big corporation. It's Jayson Woodbridge, founder of cult Cabernet brand Hundred Acre, who just closed on his fourth notable purchase in as many years.Woodbridge has acquired Calistoga's Kelly Fleming Wines, a nearly 300-acre estate with 12 acres of vines; a home; a winery built from stone in the style of southern France; and 7,000 square feet of caves.
