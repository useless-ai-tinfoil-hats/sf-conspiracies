Developer wants to ‘supersize’ S.F. project under new state housing law — while it’s being built
-----
Oakland developer oWow is the latest builder planning to "supersize" a previously approved project in San Francisco due to a recent change in state law.An application filed with the city's Planning Department on Wednesday proposes updates to the design of 960 Howard St. in the Central SoMa neighborhood, which, in recent years, was approved for redevelopment into a three-story creative office building with a nine-story, 113-unit residential addition.
