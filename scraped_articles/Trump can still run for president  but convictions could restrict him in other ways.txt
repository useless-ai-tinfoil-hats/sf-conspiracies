Donald Trump's 34 felony convictions won't prevent him from running for president, or from taking office if he wins. But they could clip his wings in other ways.Probably the foremost is travel. A number of countries, including the United Kingdom, Canada, China, Japan, India and Israel, prohibit entry by convicted felons. Unless Trump's convictions are overturned on appeal, he would be barred from visiting those nations.
