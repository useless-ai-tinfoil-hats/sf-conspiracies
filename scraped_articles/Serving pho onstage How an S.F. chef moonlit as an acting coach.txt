Serving pho onstage? How an S.F. chef moonlit as an acting coach
-----
Hovering over a stockpot that could fit a child, brandishing a ladle the size of a cereal bowl, chef Rob Lam told the cast of "My Home on the Moon" that his method of making pho is not traditional.At Richmond District eatery Lily on Clement Street, famous for "douchebag fried rice," Lam charges $42 for a bowl of pho, which he elevates with ingredients such as braised oxtail, Australian wagyu beef and broth made over the course of three days via a clarifying technique with egg whites
