Trial of man accused of assaulting ex-S.F. official reaches pivotal moment
-----
Closing arguments were scheduled to begin Tuesday morning in the trial of Garret Doty, an unhoused man charged with assaulting a former San Francisco fire commissioner last April in a case that has rippled across the city's social fabric.Doty faces three assault and battery charges for an alleged April 7 beating in the Marina District that left Don Carmignani, a former city fire commissioner, with 51 stitches, a fractured skull and a broken jaw.
Doty pleaded not guilty.
