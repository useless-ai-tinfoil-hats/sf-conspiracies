How the widow of Harris Ranch's founder started an SF steakhouse icon
-----
Stepping inside Harris' Restaurant in San Francisco feels like entering a sacred community where dry-aged steak is religion. A massive cooler affixed to the storefront window acts as the restaurant's shrine, where 32-pound slabs of marbled beef seduce the masses, beckoning them to inquire within.On its 40th anniversary, not much has changed since the storied steakhouse opened at the corner of Van Ness and Pacific avenues in 1984.
