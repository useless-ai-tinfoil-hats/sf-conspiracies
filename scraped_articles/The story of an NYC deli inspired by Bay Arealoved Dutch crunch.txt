The story of an NYC deli inspired by Bay Area-loved Dutch crunch
-----
If there's any bread other than sourdough that epitomizes the Bay Area, it's Dutch crunch.For the uninitiated, the dense, white sandwich roll is caked in rice flour, which transforms its crusty top into bronzed, imperfect cracks during baking. As a result, Dutch crunch often looks like a patchwork of islands. Or sunshine-cracked pavement. Either way, it's a divisive bread.
