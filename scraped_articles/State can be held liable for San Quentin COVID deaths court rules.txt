State can be held liable for San Quentin COVID deaths, court rules
-----
The state can be held responsible for the deaths of San Quentin inmates from COVID-19 after the transfer of infected inmates from another prison, a federal appeals court ruled Tuesday.San Quentin had no reported COVID cases until May 2020, when state prison officials transferred 122 aging or medically vulnerable inmates there from the California Institution for Men in Chino, where an outbreak of the virus had killed at least nine inmates and infected more than 600.
