Late Cal, NFL quarterback Joe Kapp had advanced CTE: ‘His brain was really under siege’
-----
Joe Kapp, the late Cal quarterback who played 12 professional seasons and relished football's high-impact collisions, was posthumously diagnosed with the most advanced form of chronic traumatic encephalopathy (CTE), his family told the Chronicle.The primary diagnosis of Stage IV CTE, a degenerative disease linked to repeated concussions and traumatic brain injuries, came from UCSF doctors who examined Kapp's brain after his death last year.
