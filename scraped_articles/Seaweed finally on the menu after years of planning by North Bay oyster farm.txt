Seaweed finally on the menu after years of planning by North Bay oyster farm
-----
The sea lettuce that blankets oyster beds in green is a natural byproduct of Tomales Bay shellfish farming. Now, after many years of planning, the edible sea lettuce and rusty-brown nori seaweed that grow there are finally being collected for culinary use.The owners of Hog Island Oyster Co., which grows oysters in the bay and runs several Bay Area restaurants, have long wanted to harvest the seaweed to put into the nori butter and hand-harvested sea salt it uses to flavor its dishes.
