Tiger cub rescued by Oakland Zoo moves to her ‘furrever home’
-----
A tiger cub with severe bone disease that was rescued from an illegal private owner by the Oakland Zoo has moved to its permanent home, a sanctuary in Northern California, zoo officials said Tuesday.On Monday, Lily the tiger cub was taken by the California Department of Fish and Wildlife from the zoo to her new home, the 2,300-acre PAWS Wildlife Sanctuary in San Andreas, a Gold Rush-era town 48 miles southwest of Stockton (San Joaquin County).
