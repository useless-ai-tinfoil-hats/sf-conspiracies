S.F. contemporary art museum finds new home in building partly owned by Trump
-----
The Institute of Contemporary Art San Francisco is relocating from its Dogpatch converted warehouse to a five-story modernist building dubbed the Cube, situated in the Financial District and partly owned by Donald Trump's family business.The move to is set for Oct. 25, just two years after opening its doors at 901 Minnesota St., and will expand the museum's exhibition space from 11,000 to 26,000 square feet.
