S.F. nonprofit scandals: New legislation promises tougher oversight amid $1.7 billion in contracts
-----
San Francisco is poised to adopt a sprawling nonprofit monitoring bill that backers say might have prevented a slew of recent scandals at organizations that provide city services.San Francisco spends about $1.7 billion a year contracting with about 600 nonprofits to offer services that include running homeless shelters, providing drug treatment and organizing neighborhood crime prevention groups.
