How an openly gay man became a trusted adviser to Martin Luther King Jr.
-----
George C. Wolfe made his mark in the theater world before turning to movies, running the Public Theater in New York from 1993 until 2004 and winning two Tony Awards for directing "Angels in America: Millennium Approaches" and "Bring in 'da Noise/Bring in 'da Funk."Growing up gay and Black in Frankfort, Ky., and finding himself in New York, he knew of Bayard Rustin, an openly gay Black man and former Broadway performer who became one of Martin Luther King Jr.'s most trusted advisers.
