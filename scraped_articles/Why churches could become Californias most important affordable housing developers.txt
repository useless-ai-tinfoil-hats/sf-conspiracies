Why churches could become California’s most important affordable housing developers
-----
On a sunny corner in North Berkeley, a new apartment building stands out among the bungalow courts, single-family Craftsman-style homes and multifamily complexes of the surrounding neighborhood. This building — containing 35 studio units for low-income seniors — is notable, not because of its architectural style, but because it was built by a church. Jordan Court is an affordable housing development built on land owned by All Souls Episcopal Parish, right next door to the church's sanctuary
