Miles of lead cables lie at the bottom of Lake Tahoe. Why are they still there?
-----
Emerald Bay, punctuated by a wooded island on Lake Tahoe's western edge, is probably the lake's most recognizable feature. But beneath the water's azure surface lies an issue few have set eyes on: about 6 miles worth of defunct century-old telephone cables that contain toxic lead.The cables, made of copper wires sheathed in lead, were discovered by scuba divers 12 years ago resting on the sandy lake bottom and, in places, bent around rocks.
