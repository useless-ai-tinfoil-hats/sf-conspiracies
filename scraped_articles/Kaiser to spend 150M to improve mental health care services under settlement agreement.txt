Kaiser to spend $150M to improve mental health care services under settlement agreement
-----
Kaiser Permanente, California's largest provider of health services, will spend $150 million over five years to improve its mental health care and pay a $50 million penalty under a settlement announced Thursday by the state.The settlement follows an investigation that the state Department of Managed Health Care began in May 2022 after complaints from patients and providers about access and oversight of behavioral health care at Kaiser facilities, the department said.
