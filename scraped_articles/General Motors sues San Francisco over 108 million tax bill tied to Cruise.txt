General Motors sues San Francisco over $108 million tax bill tied to Cruise
-----
General Motors has amended a lawsuit against San Francisco, alleging that the city unfairly imposed $108 million in taxes on the company over seven years. The Detroit-based automaker argues that the assessment was wrong, given its limited presence in terms of sales and personnel within the city.GM contends that the city erroneously associated its tax liability with its San Francisco-based Cruise driverless car division, linking it to $3 billion in global revenue.
