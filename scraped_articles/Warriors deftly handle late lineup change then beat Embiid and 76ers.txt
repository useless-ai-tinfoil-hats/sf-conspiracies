Warriors deftly handle late lineup change, then beat Embiid and 76ers
-----
Golden State Warriors coach Steve Kerr wanted to stick with the 15th starting lineup he's played this season, but an illness Tuesday to Klay Thompson altered his pregame plan.The 16th starting lineup, however, was equally effective in subduing the Philadelphia 76ers.
With rookie guard Brandin Podziemski replacing Thompson in the starting lineup alongside Stephen Curry, Andrew Wiggins, Jonathan Kuminga and Draymond Green, the Warriors capped a four-game homestand at Chase Center
