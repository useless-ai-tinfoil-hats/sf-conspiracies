‘Tow it, baby’: Some hard-core Sausalito anchor outs embrace new life on land
-----
In late January, after 23 years among the saltiest of Sausalito's anchor outs, Drew Warner rowed his dingy to Schoonmaker Beach for the last time. All he carried was a bag of clothes and his tabby cat Charlie.He had given away his anchor gear and rope, his generator and other supplies. He turned his skiff and a kayak over to the Richardson Bay Regional Agency, along with the 26-foot sailboat that had been his home for the past five years.
