Sharks allow 10 goals again, fall to Penguins in NHL's second-worst start ever
-----
The start to the San Jose Sharks season is so bad, it has waded into historic territory. Saturday's 10-2 loss to the Penguins cemented an 0-10-1 record, tied for the second-worst start in NHL history.Last year's Sharks squad finished with the fourth-worst record in the NHL, and this year's version of the team appears to be a shell of that. In the return of last season's Norris winner, Erik Karlsson, the Sharks issues were expounded on, allowing 10 goals on 35 shots.
