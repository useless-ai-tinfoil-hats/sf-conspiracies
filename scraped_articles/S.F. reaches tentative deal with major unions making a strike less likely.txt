S.F. reaches tentative deal with major unions, making a strike less likely
-----
San Francisco has reached tentative labor contracts with several of its largest unions, reducing the chances of a widespread public-sector strike as the city tries to close a huge budget deficit during a mayoral election year.Full details of the agreements were not immediately available, but SEIU Local 1021, San Francisco's largest public-sector union, said its contract would establish a $25 per hour minimum wage for city workers, among other provisions.
