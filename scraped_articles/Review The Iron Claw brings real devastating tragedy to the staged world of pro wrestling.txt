Review: ‘The Iron Claw’ brings real, devastating tragedy to the staged world of pro wrestling
-----
You don't have to fall for pro wrestling to believe in "The Iron Claw."The latest feature from Sean Durkin, a keen observer of familial dysfunction and the acclaimed writer-director of 2011's "Martha Marcy May Marlene" and 2020's "The Nest," looks with dead serious empathy at true, absurd tragedy in a world built on preposterous artifice.
It's the story of the Von Erichs, the first family of Texas wrestling and the faux sport's equivalent of the Kennedys in politics or the Allmans
