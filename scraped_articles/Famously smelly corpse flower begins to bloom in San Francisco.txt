Famously smelly corpse flower begins to bloom in San Francisco
-----
The rare corpse flower at the California Academy of Sciences has begun to bloom, officials announced Tuesday afternoon on X.The flower, named Mirage, will bloom overnight and last just one to three days. When in bloom, a corpse flower can grow as high as 10 feet and release a smell reminiscent of decaying flesh, "rotten fish, garlic, and sweaty feet," according to a California Academy of Sciences news release.
