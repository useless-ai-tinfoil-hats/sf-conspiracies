DOJ sues software company over rental price fixing, a practice S.F. just banned
-----
The Biden administration, California and seven other states sued a property-management company Friday for providing software that allegedly enables landlords to inflate rents and avoid competition. Last month, San Francisco became the first U.S. city to ban the practice.The suit, filed in North Carolina, accuses Texas-based RealPage of violating antitrust law by colluding with rental property owners, who share information they obtain from customers and use to set rents.
