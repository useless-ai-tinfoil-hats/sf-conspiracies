In 2009, the philosopher and social theorist Judith Butler wrote a book that crystallized one of the most difficult moral questions: Why do some deaths matter to us while others don't? Why do we disturb the heavens with our wailing when some die while countless others perish with only the barest of mention?The book is called "Frames of War," and it landed in 2009, at the height of the post-9/11 U.S. military involvement in Iraq and Afghanistan.
