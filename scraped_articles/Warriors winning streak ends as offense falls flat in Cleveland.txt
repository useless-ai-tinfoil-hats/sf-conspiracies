Warriors’ winning streak ends as offense falls flat in Cleveland
-----
CLEVELAND — The Golden State Warriors got outworked in the paint against the Cleveland Cavaliers on Sunday, to put it gently. That 58-24 disparity was arguably the biggest factor as the offense stalled out in a 115-104 defeat.After scoring a season-high 141 points in Oklahoma City on Friday, the Warriors shot 35.8% from the field, were outscored 26-10 in transition and were outrebounded 54-44.
It added up to Golden State's first road loss of the season following a 4-0 start away
