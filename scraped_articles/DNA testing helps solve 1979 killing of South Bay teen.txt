DNA testing helps solve 1979 killing of South Bay teen
-----
New DNA testing has helped South Bay officials identify a suspect in the killing of an 18-year-old security guard that went unsolved for 44 years.Estella Elena Mena, 18, was working as a security guard at a Sunnyvale business on Oct. 27, 1979, when she was stabbed multiple times, according to the Sunnyvale Department of Public Safety. Authorities found her in the lobby of the building and took her to a hospital, but Mena died from her injuries.
