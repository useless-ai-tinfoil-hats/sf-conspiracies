Review: In ‘The Boy and the Heron,’ Japanese animation master beats Disney at its own game
-----
In July, as American moviegoers were experiencing a view of World War II in Christopher Nolan's "Oppenheimer," which climaxed with the test of the precursor to the atomic bombs that would detonate over Hiroshima and Nagasaki, cinemagoers in Japan were seeing a different side of the war."The Boy and the Heron," the 12th and quite possibly final animated film from 82-year-old master Hayao Miyazaki that debuted in his country over the summer, begins with the horrific firebombing of Tokyo.
