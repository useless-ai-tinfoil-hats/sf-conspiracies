California agrees to stop requiring police officers to disclose their gender identity
-----
California's attorney general has agreed to stop requiring police in the state to disclose their own gender identity when they report traffic stops to an antidiscrimination board.A state law aimed at halting racial profiling by law enforcement requires officers to record information about people they stop and submit the data to the state's Racial and Identity Profiling Advisory Board, or RIPA, which analyzes the information in annual reports.
