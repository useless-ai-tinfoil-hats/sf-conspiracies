S.F.'s fatal overdoses drop again, a hopeful sign for devastating fentanyl crisis
-----
In a hopeful sign that San Francisco's devastating overdose crisis may be turning a corner, the city saw the number of fatal overdoses fall for the second consecutive month — to 39 in July. That's the lowest single-month figure since the city began releasing monthly figures in January 2020. July's numbers follow a decline seen in June, when 49 people died of overdoses — the lowest monthly number in nearly two years."We're cautiously optimistic," Dr. Grant Colfax, director of health said
