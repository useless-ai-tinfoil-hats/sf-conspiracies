Fentanyl-laced letter sent to Northern California elections department
-----
A Yuba County elections worker this week opened an envelope addressed to the office that contained a powdery substance that tested positive for fentanyl, prompting an investigation by local and state officials.The incident Tuesday involved mail that did not appear suspicious and was addressed from a verified sender. The staff member took precautionary measures, did not touch the substance and was unharmed, officials said.
