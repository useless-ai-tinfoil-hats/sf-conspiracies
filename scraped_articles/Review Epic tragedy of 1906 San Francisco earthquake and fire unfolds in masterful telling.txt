Review: Epic tragedy of 1906 San Francisco earthquake and fire unfolds in masterful telling
-----
Much of "The Longest Minute," Matthew J. Davenport's masterful, granular, ground-level history of the 1906 San Francisco earthquake and fire, unfolds like a slow-motion nightmare.The ground turns to liquid as the haphazardly constructed "fill land" beneath the streets — sand, dirt, garbage, mud — gurgles upward. Houses and buildings erected on this land collapse instantly, top stories pitching forward to street level, crushing everything and everyone beneath. Too many fires to count
