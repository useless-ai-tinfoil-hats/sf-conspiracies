10-year Treasury yield: What it is and why it matters
-----
A Treasury note, or T-note, is a U.S. government security that pays a fixed interest rate for two, three, five, seven, or 10 years. You receive regularly scheduled interest payments until maturity, at which time the government pays back the amount you originally invested (the principal).
