A century-old Japanese grilled chicken vendor is reborn in S.F.
-----
A legendary Tokyo yakitori shop that drew long lines for nearly a century is about to be re-created in San Francisco.Edomasa started as a tiny wooden yakitori cart in 1924. It became one of Tokyo's most sought-after destinations with people waiting hours for chef Masaji Watanabe's charcoal-grilled chicken skewers. After its closure last year, EK Food Services, the Japanese restaurant group behind popular local ramen chain Marufuku, decided to revive it.
