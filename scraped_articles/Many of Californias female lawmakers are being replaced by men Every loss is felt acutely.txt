Many of California’s female lawmakers are being replaced by men: ‘Every loss is felt acutely’
-----
WASHINGTON — California, known for its leadership in sending women to Congress, is seeing some of its pioneering female politicians being replaced by men, a trend worsened by the March 5 primary.California has elected more women to Congress than any other state — 46 to the House and four to the Senate since 1923 (former Sen. Barbara Boxer is the only one who has been a member of both bodies). The vast majority — 41 — have been elected in the past four decades.
