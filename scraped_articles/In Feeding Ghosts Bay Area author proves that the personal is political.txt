In ‘Feeding Ghosts,’ Bay Area author proves that the personal is political

When Tessa Hulls was growing up in the Bay Area, her grandmother was a mostly floating presence, a shell of a person who spent her days shut in her room scribbling her life story on paper, over and over. What Hulls knew of her was summed up in three vague, terse ideas: China, writer, mental illness.In recent months, as her own mother begins to succumb to dementia, Hulls has spent time back home in Point Reyes sorting through files.
