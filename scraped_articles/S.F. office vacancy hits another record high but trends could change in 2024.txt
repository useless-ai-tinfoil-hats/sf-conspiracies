S.F. office vacancy hits another record high, but trends could change in 2024
-----
San Francisco's amount of vacant office space has reached the highest level ever recorded in the city's history.According to preliminary fourth quarter data provided by real estate brokerage CBRE, the city's vacancy rate ticked up nearly 2 percentage points from the previous quarter, reaching 35.9% at year-end. In September, that number was at 33.9%, which was also the previous record high.
