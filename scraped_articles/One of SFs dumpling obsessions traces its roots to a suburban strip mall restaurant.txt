One of SF's dumpling obsessions traces its roots to a suburban strip mall restaurant
-----
"First of all, I like foods with bright colors," declared Willy Ng, co-owner of Cantonese seafood restaurant Koi Palace. We were seated in a private dining room in the ornate 400-seat Daly City restaurant, which is flanked by an Outback Steakhouse and an endodontics practice, talking about how Ng's 28-year-old restaurant was likely the first in the Bay Area, back in 2015, to offer wildly popular colored soup dumplings on its menu.
