Homelessness surged in this Bay Area county, and three other takeaways from the latest data
-----
The number of people experiencing homelessness rose in San Francisco over the past two years, but new data shows it surged even more in a nearby Bay Area county.Officials tallied 2,130 unhoused people in San Mateo County during a "point-in-time," or PIT, count in January. That reflected a roughly 18% increase from the 1,808 people counted during the 2022 survey, growth that stood in contrast to what appeared to be a much smaller uptick region-wide.
