Oakland to spend $2.5 million to improve struggling 911 system, hire dispatchers
-----
Oakland Mayor Sheng Thao on Monday announced additional funding to help improve its 911 dispatch center, which has grappled with slow response times and a high vacancy rate as the city sees increasing crime.The city is directing $2.5 million over the next two years to hire more dispatchers and upgrade the computer-aided dispatch systems, which dispatchers use to connect emergency responders to a call. Officials urged the public to apply to these jobs.
