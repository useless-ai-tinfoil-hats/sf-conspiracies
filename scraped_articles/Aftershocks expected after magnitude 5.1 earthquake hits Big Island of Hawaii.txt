Aftershocks expected after magnitude 5.1 earthquake hits Big Island of Hawaii
-----
A magnitude 5.1 earthquake shook the Big Island of Hawaii on Monday evening with an epicenter at the Kilauea Volcano, about 9 miles outside the town of Volcano, according to the U.S. Geological Survey. There was no risk of a tsunami, Hawaii's Emergency Management Agency said.The quake was felt across the island, and 1,100 people reported feeling shaking with the USGS. It hit at approximately 5:54 p.m. local time with a depth of about 1 mile below sea level.
