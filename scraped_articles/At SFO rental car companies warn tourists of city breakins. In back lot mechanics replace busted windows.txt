At SFO, rental car companies warn tourists of city break-ins. In back lot, mechanics replace busted windows
-----
When Scarlet Kenwrick and Philippa Haile arrived at San Francisco International Airport this month, they checked out their Nissan Rogue from the Alamo Rent a Car kiosk and received a stern warning from the clerk.Whatever you do, hide your valuables in your car, the clerk, whose son recently lost his bags when his car was burglarized, warned the friends visiting California from England for a road trip.
"She put the fear of God in us," Haile laughed Saturday as the pair stood outside
