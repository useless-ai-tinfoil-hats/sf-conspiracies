Letters: Sale of Coliseum won’t fix Oakland’s budget problem. Here’s why
-----
Regarding "Oakland reaches landmark deal to sell Coliseum site to developers, ending decades of ownership" (East Bay, SFChronicle.com, May 22): Whatever the reality and timeframe of Oakland's sale of its share of the Coliseum, using one-time revenues to bail out structural budget deficits is a terrible idea.Sometimes cities get hit with major exogenous forces, such as COVID, and have few short-term choices, but using one-time revenue as a budget patch just kicks the can down the road.
