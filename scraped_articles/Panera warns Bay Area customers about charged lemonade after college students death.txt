Panera warns Bay Area customers about ‘charged’ lemonade after college student’s death
-----
Panera Bread, the popular fast-casual restaurant chain, has posted a new warning in its Bay Area restaurants about its Charged Lemonade products following the death of a 21-year-old woman.Sarah Katz's family filed a lawsuit in Philadelphia last week, claiming that the Ivy League student suffered cardiac arrest and died after consuming the beverage, which reportedly contains more caffeine than a 12-ounce Red Bull and a 16-ounce Monster Energy Drink combined.
