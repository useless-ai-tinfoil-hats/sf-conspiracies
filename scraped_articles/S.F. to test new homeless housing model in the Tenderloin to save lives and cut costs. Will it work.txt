S.F. to test new homeless housing model in the Tenderloin to save lives and cut costs. Will it work?
-----
After 38 years in prison, Wesley Clutchett found himself homeless for the first time in his late 70s. He was dangerously thin, living out of his car and suffering from late-stage cancer he didn't know he had.That's when Clutchett was given a room in a converted Oakland hotel and selected to take part in a new pilot program designed to provide skilled nursing and in-home health care to homeless people with serious medical conditions.
