Tragic death raises troubling questions about safety on Yosemite's Half Dome
-----
More than a few hikers have faced dire consequences in pursuit of Yosemite National Park's iconic Half Dome summit. Some have gone into cardiac arrest. Others have been struck by lightning. Still more have tumbled into ice-cold rivers, fainted on the ascent or slipped on wet granite along the infamous cable system.Over a decade ago, Yosemite officials created a permit system in hopes that limiting the number of people who could attempt the climb at once would mitigate some of the danger.
