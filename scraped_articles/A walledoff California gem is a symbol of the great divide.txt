A walled-off California gem is a symbol of the great divide
-----
In 1880, railroad tycoon Charles Crocker opened the Hotel Del Monte in the heart of Monterey. A year after, Crocker, along with fellow tycoons Leland Stanford, Collis Potter Huntington and Mark Hopkins, introduced a stunning natural attraction in the form of an open path for visitors to traverse the spectacular nearby coastline. It was meant to lure folks from the Midwest and East Coast onto their rail lines for an adventure out west.They called it 17-Mile Drive.
