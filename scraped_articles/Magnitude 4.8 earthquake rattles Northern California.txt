Magnitude 4.8 earthquake rattles Northern California
-----
A magnitude 4.8 earthquake rattled California's Humboldt County early Monday morning and was followed by three aftershocks, according to the U.S. Geological Survey.The bigger quake struck 12 miles east of Petrolia with a depth of 19 miles at 3:20 a.m., the USGS said. Petrolia, a town of about 1,000 people, is 5 miles from the ocean. The epicenter was 35 miles south of the larger town of Eureka.
