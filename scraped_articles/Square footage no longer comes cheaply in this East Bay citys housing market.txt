Square footage no longer comes cheaply in this East Bay city’s housing market
-----
When homebuyers started flooding into Fremont, many real estate agents pointed out their clients were getting much more space for their money than they would in cities such as San Francisco.But that no longer appears to be the case. The East Bay suburb's homes sold for a median of $960 per square foot in March — $1 more than the price in San Francisco, according to data from real estate brokerage Redfin.
