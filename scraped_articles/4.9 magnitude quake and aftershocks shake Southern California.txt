4.9 magnitude quake and aftershocks shake Southern California
-----
A magnitude 4.9 earthquake rattled Southern California on Monday afternoon.The first quake was reported at 1 p.m. with an epicenter in the Barstow area of the Mojave Desert, approximately 114 miles northeast of Los Angeles. The earthquake occurred at a depth of 4.5 miles, according to the United States Geological Survey.
At least two aftershocks of magnitude 3.5 and 2.7 followed the initial quake.
