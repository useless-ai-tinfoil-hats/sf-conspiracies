Oakland businesses shut their doors in protest over crime and public safety
-----
Some Oakland businesses went on strike Tuesday, shutting their doors for two hours to protest the city's public safety response and call attention to their demand for more state and federal resources to combat crime.Carl Chan, a public safety advocate who lives in Alameda, said the business community wants the state, the Sheriff's Office, and the FBI and ATF to help the city's police department fight crime. The strike took place from 10 a.m. to noon.
