Road to Rickwood: How Birmingham’s segregation affected nation’s oldest ballpark
-----
White fans were separated from Black fans at Rickwood Field, where everyone knew the locations of the "colored grandstand" and "colored bleachers," among the most telling and repulsive symbols of life in segregated Birmingham.For decades, including during Willie Mays' time with the Negro Leagues' Black Barons, that was life at Rickwood. Segregated seating beyond right field divided fans by race, reflecting life in the city's neighborhoods, restaurants, schools and playgrounds.
