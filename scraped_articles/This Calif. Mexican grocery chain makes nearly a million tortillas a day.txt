This Calif. Mexican grocery chain makes nearly a million tortillas a day
-----
The tortilla machine at Vallarta Supermarkets in Pasadena is working on high speed, shooting out disc after flattened disc made with fresh masa. Some tortillas are stacked and packaged to sell; others are cut and fried to a perfect golden crisp before being bagged as chips ready to scoop up salsa, guacamole and the like.That magical machine — and others like it at the 55 Vallarta Supermarkets locations in California — whips out up to 20,000 tortillas a day.
