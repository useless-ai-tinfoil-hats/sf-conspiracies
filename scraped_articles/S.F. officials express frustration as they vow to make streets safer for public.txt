S.F. officials express frustration as they vow to make streets safer for public
-----
Supervisor Matt Dorsey, District Attorney Brooke Jenkins and other San Francisco officials struck a cautiously optimistic tone on Monday evening while fielding residents' questions on crime, homelessness and open-air drug dealing, laying out an array of law enforcement plans to combat the city's most urgent public safety concerns.The officials met with a packed auditorium of lively and frustrated — but mostly receptive — San Franciscans at a public safety town hall at the Strand theater
