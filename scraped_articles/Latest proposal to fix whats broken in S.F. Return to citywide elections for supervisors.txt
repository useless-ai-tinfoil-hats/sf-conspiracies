Latest proposal to fix what’s broken in S.F.: Return to citywide elections for supervisors
-----
Longtime San Francisco politico Quentin Kopp has proposed a ballot measure that could dramatically upend the city's Board of Supervisors — and the way voters choose who sits on it.Last week, he filed a ballot measure to amend the City Charter to have district supervisors elected in what is essentially an at-large process.
The ballot measure would make all registered voters eligible to vote for candidates in specified districts, he said.
