Why people line up outside this small Bay Area grocery store every afternoon
-----
Every day just before 4 p.m., a line starts forming outside of Suruki Supermarket in San Mateo. Once the clock strikes the hour, the small Japanese grocery store opens the floodgates for its daily sale: sushi, bento boxes, onigiri and other prepared foods are all 50% off.The line sometimes grows as long as 30 or 40 people deep. Typically, Suruki is sold out in less than 15 minutes.
