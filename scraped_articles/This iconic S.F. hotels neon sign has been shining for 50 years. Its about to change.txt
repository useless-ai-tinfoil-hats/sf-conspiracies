This iconic S.F. hotel’s neon sign has been shining for 50 years. It’s about to change
-----
The Palace Hotel has dominated the corner of San Francisco's Market and New Montgomery streets since 1873. Rebuilt after the 1906 earthquake, it's a stately block topped, incongruously, by two roof-level neon signs that date back at least 60 years.Now, those signs are cloaked in black netting — but they're not coming down.
