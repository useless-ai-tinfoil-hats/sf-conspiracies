Northern California family pleads guilty to selling $38 million worth of stolen catalytic converters
-----
A Sacramento mother and her two sons pleaded guilty Monday to charges related to their roles in a nationwide catalytic converter crime ring that netted $38 million over three years, federal prosecutors said.Monica Moua, 51, Tou Sue Vang, 31, and Andrew Vang, 27, admitted to conspiring to transport stolen catalytic converters from California to New Jersey in return for millions of dollars in wired payments, prosecutors said. Tou Sue Vang also pleaded guilty to an additional 39 charges
