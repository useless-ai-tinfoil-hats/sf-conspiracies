S.F. gets $8 million federal grant to improve Tenderloin pedestrian safety
-----
The federal government has awarded San Francisco an $8 million grant to make streets in the Tenderloin safer for pedestrians, Sen. Alex Padilla announced.The project was chosen because pedestrians are 10 times more likely to be injured or killed by a car in the Tenderloin than elsewhere in the city, federal officials said Wednesday.
