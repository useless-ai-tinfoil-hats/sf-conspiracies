S.F.’s FiDi is getting a storefront architecture center — can it help revive the neighborhood?
-----
As civic leaders float visions of soccer stadiums and university campuses as ways to revive central San Francisco, a storefront in the Financial District shows another way that downtowns can rebound.There's a coffee bar with a few chairs along a sumptuous table of reclaimed elm, and a lecture hall with 20-foot-walls clad in Douglas Fir. White walls reminiscent of art galleries run behind thick structural columns showing the worn ambiance of age.
