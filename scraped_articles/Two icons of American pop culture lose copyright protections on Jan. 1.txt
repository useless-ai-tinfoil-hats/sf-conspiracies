Two icons of American pop culture lose copyright protections on Jan. 1
-----
Every Jan. 1, a new batch of books, movies and other artistic creations enters the public domain. Copyright law in the United States is a complicated matter, but at its simplest, what's happening this year is this: As of Jan. 1, 2024, the copyright expires on works from 1928 and they enter the public domain.The most famous — and controversial — of this year's batch are two icons of American pop culture: Mickey and Minnie Mouse.
