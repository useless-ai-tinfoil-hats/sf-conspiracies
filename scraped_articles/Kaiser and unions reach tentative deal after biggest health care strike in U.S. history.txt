Kaiser and unions reach tentative deal after biggest health care strike in U.S. history
-----
Oakland-based Kaiser Permanente reached a tentative agreement Friday with the coalition of unions representing 75,000 of its employees following what was considered the largest strike of health care workers in U.S. history.The agreement brings to an end months of contentious negotiations between California's largest health care provider and workers increasingly frustrated with burnout and short-staffing exacerbated by the pandemic.
