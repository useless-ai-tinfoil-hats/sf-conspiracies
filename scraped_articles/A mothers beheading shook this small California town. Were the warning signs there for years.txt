LITTLE RIVER, Mendocino County — The night before the shooting and killing and roaring flames changed everything, Asahn Smith got a text from a friend he hadn't spoken with in years. Fletcher Pinkham, his old hip-hop songwriting pal, had a new tune called "Cannabis Cowboy.""I can show you how to grow that fire," rang the chorus.
"The song — it's super-catchy," Smith told the Chronicle.
It may have also been horribly prescient.
