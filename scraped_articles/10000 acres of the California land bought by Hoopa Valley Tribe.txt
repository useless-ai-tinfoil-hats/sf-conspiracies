10,000 acres of the California land bought by Hoopa Valley Tribe
-----
A wide swath of rolling pine forests and rushing rivers in Northern California has been returned to a Native American tribe in a landmark $14.1 million deal.The 10,395-acre Pine Creek Tract in Humboldt County has been acquired by the Hoopa Valley Tribe, the tribe announced Wednesday. The land, around 20 miles northeast of Eureka, borders the western boundary of the tribe's reservation, and was sold by Australian forest management company New Forests.
