Sex trafficking ring operated brothels in ‘low budget’ Bay Area hotels, prosecutors say
-----
South Bay prosecutors announced charges this week against nine people accused of running what officials called a "Colombian-based sex trafficking ring" that operated brothels in hotels across the Bay Area.Eighteen sex trafficking victims, many from countries in South America, were rescued and released to the care of local aid groups, Santa Clara County District Attorney Jeff Rosen said. One teenage victim was taken to the Children's Advocacy Center for care.
