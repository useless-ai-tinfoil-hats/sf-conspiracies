50 fired Google workers file labor complaint, allege illegal retaliation over in-office protest
-----
Fifty Google workers who were fired after a pro-Palestine demonstration inside work offices earlier this month have filed a complaint with the National Labor Relations Board, alleging that the tech giant "unlawfully retaliated" against them.The fired workers are seeking reinstatement to their jobs, back pay, and guarantees from Google leadership that the company won't retaliate against workers for "lawful collective protest," according to Jane Chung, a spokesperson for the protesters.
