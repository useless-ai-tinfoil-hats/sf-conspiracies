California’s all-electric shift is ramping up. Here’s what will start vanishing from shelves in January
-----
When Palo Alto banned gas-powered leaf blowers in 2005, professional landscaper Humberto Vasquez was wary.He adjusted reluctantly, adding electric blowers to the fleet of his firm, Bayfront Landscape, but charging clients more than in places where gas was still an option. In his experience, he said, blowers with batteries were less powerful, so the work took longer.
And while the technology has improved over the last 20 years, he still prefers gas-powered models.
