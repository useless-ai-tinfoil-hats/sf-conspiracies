College sophomore suspected of setting multiple fires at San Jose State campus
-----
A San Jose State University student was arrested on suspicion of setting a string of fires on the school's campus over several days, Cal Fire spokespersons said.The suspect was identified as Amogh Upadhya, a sophomore at the university. He was arrested and charged with four counts of arson, officials said Thursday.
Upadhya was being held at the Santa Clara County Jail on a $400,000 bail until his court hearing Friday.
