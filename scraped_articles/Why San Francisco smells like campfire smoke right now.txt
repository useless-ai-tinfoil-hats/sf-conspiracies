Why San Francisco smells like campfire smoke right now
-----
A thick blanket of wildfire smoke covered parts of the San Francisco Bay Area on Tuesday, and the National Weather Service confirmed that northerly winds were pushing smoke from fires in far Northern California into the region.Sarah McCorkle, a forecaster with the weather service's Bay Area office, told SFGATE that the winds are between 2,000 to 5,000 feet high in the atmosphere, and smoke is mixing down from upper levels of the atmosphere toward the Earth's surface.
