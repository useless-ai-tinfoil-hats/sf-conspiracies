S.F. arrests drug users hoping to lead them to sobriety. The city doesn't know if it's working
-----
A year after San Francisco launched a multi-agency effort to dismantle open-air drug markets in the Tenderloin and South of Market neighborhoods, it remains unclear whether arrests have led any drug users to engage in treatment programs.The Chronicle spoke to several unhoused people who use fentanyl and who were recently arrested or cited, all of whom said it had not affected their drug habits.
