Two detectives are behind two wrongful convictions. Why is S.F. still defending them?
-----
One afternoon in April 1990, George Varela sat in a police interrogation room facing a chilling predicament.The 18-year-old had recently been the getaway driver in a San Francisco homicide, and the two city detectives questioning him said they had evidence to prove it. But they reassured the teenager that their priority was prosecuting the man who pulled the trigger.
The detectives thought the killer was a man whose name had emerged in a street rumor within hours of the shooting.
