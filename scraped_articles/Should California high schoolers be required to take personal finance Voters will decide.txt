Should California high schoolers be required to take personal finance? Voters will decide
-----
California voters can expect to decide in November whether high school students will have to take a personal finance course in order to graduate, a ballot initiative already drawing vocal support and opposition.The secretary of state announced Wednesday that the measure is eligible for the ballot, with a sufficient number of signatures to qualify, with official qualification in late June unless withdrawn by the proponents.
