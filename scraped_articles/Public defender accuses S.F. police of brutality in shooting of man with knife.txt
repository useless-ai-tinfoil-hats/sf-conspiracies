Public defender accuses S.F. police of brutality in shooting of man with knife
-----
The San Francisco Public Defender's Office condemned the shooting of a San Francisco man in the Tenderloin district last month as an "act of police brutality."Police shot and injured the man on Aug. 28 after he refused their orders, moved toward them and pulled out a large knife, according to police body camera video released last week.
The Public Defender's Office said police should have de-escalated the situation.
