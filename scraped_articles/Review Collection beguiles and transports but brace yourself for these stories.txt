Review: Collection beguiles and transports, but brace yourself for these stories

Reading Irish-born Claire Keegan is like succumbing to a drug: eerie, hallucinogenic, time-stopping. Her simplest sentences envelop the brain (and all the senses) in a deep, fully dimensional dream.The prize-winning Keegan may now be best known to Americans by the acclaimed film version of her story "Foster" (released in the Irish language as "The Quiet Girl") or by her last novel, "Small Things Like These," shortlisted for the Booker Prize in 2022.
