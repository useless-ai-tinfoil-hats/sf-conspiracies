Jazz legend Dave Brubeck's Modernist masterpiece floats above the Oakland hills
-----
When Dave Brubeck was discharged from the army after World War II, his dad presented him with a $1,000 war bond as a welcome-home gift. The jazz pianist used the modest nest egg to purchase a small plot of land near the peak of the Oakland Hills in 1949, years before he became a household name.At first, Brubeck and his wife Iola couldn't afford to build a home on the 50-by-100 foot lot, which was steep and heavily wooded.
