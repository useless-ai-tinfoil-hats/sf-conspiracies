How four rangers survive winter in a California ghost town
-----
A heavy snowstorm in 1859 killed the town's founder and harsh winters continue to plague the California ghost town Bodie State Historic Park.The former gold-mining settlement, some 300 miles east of San Francisco, was left just as it was when its townspeople abandoned it in the late 1800s.
State Park rangers remain on site throughout the winter for a simple reason; even when temperatures drop and the windchill can burn the skin, somebody still comes nearly every day to visit. Read
