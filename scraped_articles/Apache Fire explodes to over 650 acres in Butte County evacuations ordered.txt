Apache Fire explodes to over 650 acres in Butte County, evacuations ordered
-----
Butte County residents in a small town were forced to evacuate Monday evening when a fast-moving wildfire broke out in Oroville, Cal Fire said.The blaze, dubbed the "Apache Fire," sparked near Apache Hill Road in Oroville around 7:21 p.m., firefighters said. Just after midnight Tuesday, the blaze remained uncontained, had torched 650 acres and destroyed two structures, Cal Fire said. Separately, two spot fires that sparked late Monday had burned another 50 acres, according to Cal Fire.
