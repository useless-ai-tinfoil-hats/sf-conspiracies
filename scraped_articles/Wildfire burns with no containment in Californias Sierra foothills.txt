Wildfire burns with no containment in California's Sierra foothills
-----
A wildfire that ignited in California's Sierra Nevada foothills early Wednesday morning triggered evacuations and pumped out smoke that was visible from Interstate 50, officials said.The Crozier Fire was first reported at 1:21 a.m. in the wilderness of El Dorado County, about 15 miles northeast of Placerville and 60 miles northeast of Sacramento, according to Cal Fire.
The fire was reported to be 25 acres just before 3:30 a.m. Wednesday morning. By 7 a.m., it had reached 67 acres
