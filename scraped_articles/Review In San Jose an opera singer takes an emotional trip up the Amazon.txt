Review: In San Jose, an opera singer takes an emotional trip up the Amazon
-----
Puccini wrote operas set in Paris, Rome, Japan, China and even Louisiana, but he never turned his attention to South America. So in 1996, nearly three-quarters of a century after Puccini's death, Mexican composer Daniel Catán stepped in to fill that gap.Catán's "Florencia en el Amazones," which opened Saturday, April 20, in a deftly executed production at Opera San José, doesn't make the faintest attempt to hide or modulate its Puccinian lineage.
