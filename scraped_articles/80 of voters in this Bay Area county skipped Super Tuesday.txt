80% of voters in this Bay Area county skipped Super Tuesday
-----
Turnout for last week's primary election was sharply down across the Bay Area, and one county lagged far behind the rest.In Alameda County, just 20.75% of voters cast a ballot as of Wednesday, representing the lowest turnout so far among the nine counties in the region. Preliminary election results from the Alameda County Registrar of Voters show that just 194,902 out of 939,274 registered voters have turned in a ballot for the March 5 election so far.
