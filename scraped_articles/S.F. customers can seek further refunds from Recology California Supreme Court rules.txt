S.F. customers can seek further refunds from Recology, California Supreme Court rules
-----
The state Supreme Court cleared the way Tuesday for San Franciscans to seek further refunds from trash-collector Recology, which has already repaid $94.5 million for excessive rates related to the bribery of former city Public Works Director Mohammed Nuru.The court unanimously denied Recology's request to hear its appeal of a lower-court ruling allowing customers to sue for additional payments for past overcharges and punitive damages for alleged bribery and fraud.
