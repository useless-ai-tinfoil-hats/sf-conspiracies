KQED offers buyout packages with layoffs potentially coming to cut costs
-----
KQED is initiating staff buyouts, an effort to reduce costs in the face of a budget deficit, SFGATE has learned.In an April 17 email to staff at the Bay Area's best-known public broadcasting station, KQED President Michael Isip said the buyouts are a first step in reducing costs and that additional tactics might be necessary, including layoffs or a hiring freeze.
