How has the Ferry Building shaped San Francisco's soul? Chronicle critic John King untangles its history
-----
In San Francisco, old buildings are often home to fascinating secrets.Such is certainly the case for the Ferry Building, a landmark location with a remarkable history. Opened in 1898, it has, over the years, managed to survive two earthquakes, the birth of the automobile and a 23-year period of forced isolation thanks to the construction of a confoundingly garish freeway.
