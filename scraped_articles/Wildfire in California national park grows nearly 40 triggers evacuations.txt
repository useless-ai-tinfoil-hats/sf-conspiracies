Wildfire in California national park grows nearly 40%, triggers evacuations
-----
A wildfire ignited by lightning on Aug. 3 in the southwest corner of Sequoia National Park has been quiet for the past week but exploded Tuesday into Wednesday amid dry, hot weather, growing more than 35% in size and triggering evacuations, officials said.The Coffee Pot Fire that started southeast of the town of Three Rivers in Tulare County grew from 3,678 acres on Tuesday to 5,044 acres by Wednesday evening, and has no containment, according to park officials.
