Review: How much would fancy Tokyo toilets in exquisite ‘Perfect Days’ cost in San Francisco?

When I visited Tokyo as a tourist in 2005, I couldn't believe how clean the city was. Even then in San Francisco, there was litter on the sidewalks, despite there being public trash cans every half block or so. In Tokyo, trash cans were few and far between (I had to walk blocks with a food wrapper in my pocket before finding a proper place to put it), yet the sidewalks were spotless.The Oscar-nominated "Perfect Days," a Japanese film made by German director Wim Wenders, gives us a clue
