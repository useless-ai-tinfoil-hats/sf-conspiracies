Preliminary magnitude 5.7 earthquake 'felt widely' on Big Island and across Hawaii
-----
An earthquake with a preliminary magnitude of 5.7 gave the Big Island a shake on Friday, according to the U.S. Geological Survey.There is no tsunami threat, the Hawaii Emergency Management Agency said.
The tremor hit at 10:06 a.m. local time with a depth of about 23 miles on the island's southwest side, near the town of Pahala and Hawaii Volcanoes National Park.
The epicenter was 45.5 miles southeast of Kailua-Kona and 45.7 miles southwest of Hilo, according to the USGS.
