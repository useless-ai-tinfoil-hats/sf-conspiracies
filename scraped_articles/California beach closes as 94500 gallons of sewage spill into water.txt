California beach closes as 94,500 gallons of sewage spill into water
-----
An estimated 94,500 gallons of untreated sewage spilled into the waters near Laguna Beach on Wednesday, leading city officials to close a 2-mile stretch of public beach in the community about an hour's drive southeast of Los Angeles.The spill began after one of the city's main sewer lines broke during a repair operation, the Orange County Health Care Agency said.
The breakage occurred in a portion of the line at Bluebird Canyon and the Pacific Coast Highway.
