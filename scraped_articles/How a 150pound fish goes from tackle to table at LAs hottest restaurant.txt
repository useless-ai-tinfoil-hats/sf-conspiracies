How a 150-pound fish goes from tackle to table at LA's hottest restaurant
-----
"It's definitely a two-person job."That's chef Ari Kolender talking about the process of carrying a locally caught Pacific bluefin tuna — which can weigh up to 200 pounds and measure around 5 feet long — through the door at the Los Angeles restaurant Queen St. The beast is massive and slippery, for starters. It's also a beast to of a job to corral it past the U-shaped marble bar and into the restaurant's tiny kitchen.
