S.F. jury acquits man accused of hate crime charges in Castro District incident
-----
A San Francisco jury acquitted a man of hate crime charges related to two June incidents near Mission Dolores Park in which he was accused of throwing glass and shouting anti-gay slurs at several men, the city's Public Defender's Office announced Wednesday.Public defenders claimed the San Francisco Police Department "misrepresented facts" and "failed to properly investigate" the incidents involving defendant Muhammed Abdullah, 21.
Abdullah was arrested and charged with two counts
