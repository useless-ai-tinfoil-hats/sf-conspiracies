It may be hard to believe now, but there was a time when public officials — and the Chronicle — wanted to tear down San Francisco's Ferry Building. Or that for 32 years it was cut off from the rest of the city by a double-deck concrete freeway.Now the 125-year-old building is revered as an irreplaceable icon, and one of the best examples of reinvention in the city's history.
