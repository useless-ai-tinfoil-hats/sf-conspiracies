Review: Cinematic opera ‘Fellow Travelers’ captures charged moment, rapturous love story during Lavender Scare
-----
It begins with a bottle of milk. That's what dewey-eyed cub reporter Timmy Laughlin is drinking, in the opening street scene of the opera "Fellow Travelers," when a stranger approaches.The man, a closeted gay State Department official aptly named "Hawk" Fuller, sits down and makes his sexual interest in the Washington, D.C. newcomer plain — to the audience if not right away to the innocent-seeming, milk-swilling Timmy. It's no wonder Hawk later names him "Skippy."
