Wildland firefighters have been breathing smoke unabated for generations with no protection like masks or respirators and scant research into their long term health. But that could change in the years ahead.California officials took steps toward a solution in late August on an arid hillside about 40 minutes outside Los Angeles. Thirty wildland firefighters pulled on standard-issue uniforms and hooked heavy backpacks carrying safety gear, tools, food and water onto their shoulders.
