Two Bay Area video game giants collectively laying off around 1,600

Two Silicon Valley video game heavyweights are both cutting jobs as the industry continues to shrink after booming during the pandemic.Electronic Arts — maker of best-selling franchises "Madden NFL," "Battlefield" and "The Sims" — said Wednesday it would cut 5% of its workforce, or around 700 workers. The Redwood City company previously cut 6% of its jobs, about a year ago.
