Northern California fire explodes overnight, grows to 10,000 acres
-----
A wind-driven wildfire in Colusa County, 100 miles northwest of Sacramento, exploded overnight, growing from 50 acres on Monday afternoon to 10,000 acres on Tuesday morning, according to Cal Fire. The Sites Fire was 15% contained as of 12:30 p.m. Tuesday.The area where the fire is burning was under a red flag warning Tuesday morning, the highest alert from the National Weather Service, signaling conditions that may result in extreme fire behavior. The warning expired at noon Tuesday.
