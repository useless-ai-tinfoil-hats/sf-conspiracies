38-year-old Bay Area Chinese restaurant to close permanently
-----
A Palo Alto Chinese restaurant is closing its storefront after 38 years.Jing Jing Gourmet, at 443 Emerson St., plans to close its doors permanently after service on May 19, according to The Mercury News. The restaurant, first opened in 1986, was known for serving both Szechuan and Hunan cuisine. Menu highlights included dry sauteed shredded beef, Hunan tofu and the Jing Jing Special Prawn in a spicy black bean sauce.
