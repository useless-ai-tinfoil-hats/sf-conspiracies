Charlie Munger’s legacy: Here’s what the investing legend said about See’s Candy
-----
Longtime Berkshire Hathaway Vice Chairman Charles Munger died Tuesday, leaving behind a legacy of investment success — one built in part by the lessons he learned from South San Francisco-based See's Candies.Munger died at the age of 99 in a California hospital Tuesday morning, the Associated Press reported. For more than five decades, Munger served as Warren Buffett's right-hand man, helping to build Berkshire Hathaway from a failing textile company into a multinational conglomerate.
