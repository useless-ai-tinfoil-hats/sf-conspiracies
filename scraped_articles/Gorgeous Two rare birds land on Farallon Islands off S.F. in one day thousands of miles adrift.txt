‘Gorgeous’: Two rare birds land on Farallon Islands off S.F. in one day, thousands of miles adrift
-----
Certain types of birds are expected at the Farallon Islands in incredible numbers this time of year. Tens of thousands of common murres, cormorants and seagulls settle down on the rocky wildlife refuge 27 miles from San Francisco to lay eggs and raise their young.But this spring, the island also hosted two extremely rare birds, both on the same day. One, a blue rock thrush, had probably made its way from Asia, while the other, a swallowtail gull, came from as far as the Galapagos
