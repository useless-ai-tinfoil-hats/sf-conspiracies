In January 1847, William Eddy showed up at Johnson's Ranch, emaciated to the point he made the woman who fed him weep. Behind him, 6 miles of snow spattered with blood from his feet led rescuers to the rest of his group. Later, rescue parties penetrated even further into the Sierra Nevada to temporary encampments around an alpine lake.
