Katie Ledecky swims to Olympic history in 1,500. Then the ‘joy comes out’
-----
PARIS — Katie Ledecky pulled ahead in the first 50 meters and set her internal metronome. Stroke. Stoke. Stroke. Lap. Lap. Lap.For 1,500 meters, she put on another clinic in her signature race. And when she won by a third of a pool length, switched off the metronome and looked up at the clock, Ledecky slapped the water excitedly, pumped her fist, and splashed again.
"I saw time I could be really happy with," she said. "I don't mean to celebrate that much, but it just comes out.
