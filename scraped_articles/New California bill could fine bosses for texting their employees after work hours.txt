New California bill could fine bosses for texting their employees after work hours
-----
A new California bill would potentially fine employers for — unless there's an emergency — repeatedly trying to get in touch with their workers after work hours.Assemblymember Matt Haney, a Democrat from San Francisco, sponsored the bill. Assembly Bill 2751 is modeled after so-called "right to disconnect" laws that are popular in other countries such as Belgium and France.
