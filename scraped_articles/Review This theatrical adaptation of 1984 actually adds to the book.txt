Review: This theatrical adaptation of ‘1984’ actually adds to the book
-----
If you last read "1984" decades ago, you might remember most vividly the doublethink and thought crimes, the notion that Big Brother is always watching, the giant book-in-a-book outlining how "ignorance is strength" and "war is peace."To encounter the story as theater, as in Michael Gene Sullivan's harrowing stage adaption, is to be reminded that George Orwell's masterpiece doesn't live merely in the realm of brainsploding theory.
