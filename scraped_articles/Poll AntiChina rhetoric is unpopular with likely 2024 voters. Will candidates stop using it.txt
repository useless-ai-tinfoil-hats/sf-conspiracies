Poll: Anti-China rhetoric is unpopular with likely 2024 voters. Will candidates stop using it?
-----
When a Texas man brutally stabbed a 6-year-old Asian child and attacked his family in March 2020, he admitted it was because he believed the family was responsible for the COVID-19 pandemic. The rhetoric mirrored that of then-President Donald Trump, who had by that point labeled the disease a "foreign virus" and endorsed calling it the "China virus."In the lead-up to this year's election, political observers say they expect increasing use of anti-China rhetoric by presidential candidates
