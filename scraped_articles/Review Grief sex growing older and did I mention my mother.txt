Review: Grief, sex, growing older and did I mention my mother?
-----
Reading Kate Christensen's incisive eighth novel, a quote from 19th century author Ivan Turgenev came to mind: "A poet must be a psychologist." As evidenced in her previous works (including 2008 PEN/Faulkner Award winner "The Great Man"), Christensen is both. Her prose glimmers and glints, more sensation than exposition, whether she's shining her light on broken family, broken dreams or our broken Earth.
