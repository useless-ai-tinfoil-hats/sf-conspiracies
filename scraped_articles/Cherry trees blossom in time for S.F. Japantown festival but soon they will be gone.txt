Cherry trees blossom in time for S.F. Japantown festival, but soon they will be gone
-----
In a lifetime of attending the Northern California Cherry Blossom Festival, Emily Murase has seldom seen the cherry trees in the Japantown Peace Plaza do what they are supposed to do, which is to blossom for the grand parade that caps the two-weekend event.But there they were Sunday, in 68-degree sunshine, 24 of them all in a row by the edge of the stage, a gentle breeze blowing their delicate pinkish petals off the branch and fluttering toward the stage.
