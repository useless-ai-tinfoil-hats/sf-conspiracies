To fix San Francisco, moderate group wants voters to add to mayor's power
-----
A moderate San Francisco advocacy group and a city supervisor have embarked on parallel efforts to reform the city government with two basic policy prescriptions: Give the mayor more power and reduce the number of commissions.Both share a belief that the structure of City Hall is itself one of the primary barriers preventing San Francisco from solving its myriad problems of homelessness, housing, drug abuse and economic strife.
