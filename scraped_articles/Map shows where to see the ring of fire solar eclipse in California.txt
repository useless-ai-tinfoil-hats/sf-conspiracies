UPDATE: Bay Area weather might ruin solar eclipse views. Here's what to expectA "ring of fire" solar eclipse will unfold this weekend — though only some parts of the U.S. will see its most dramatic effects.
The event on Saturday, Oct. 14 — called an annular eclipse because the moon blocks all but the sun's fiery outer edge, or annulus — differs from a full solar eclipse because of the relative position of the moon to our planet, astronomers said.
