No bids for dozens of S.F. apartment buildings clears way for real estate giant’s takeover
-----
There were no bidders on Thursday during the auction for a huge portfolio of 62 distressed San Francisco apartment buildings — but a winner still emerged.The auctioneer said that the "beneficiary" behind the defaulted mortgages would take over the properties, which had been owned by Veritas. Property records show that Brookfield, the Canadian conglomerate that also owns the Pier 70 and 5M projects, will now seize control of the buildings.
