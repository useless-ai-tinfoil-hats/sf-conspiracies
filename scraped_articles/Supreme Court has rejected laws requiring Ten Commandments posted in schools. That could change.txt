Supreme Court has rejected laws requiring Ten Commandments posted in schools. That could change
-----
The constitutional "wall of separation between church and state," as declared by Thomas Jefferson in 1802, has become much more fragile in recent years due to rulings by the U.S. Supreme Court. The wall could be further crumbled, or even toppled, by a new Louisiana law that would bring the Ten Commandments into public school classrooms.In 1980, the court ruled that a Kentucky law requiring the Ten Commandments to be displayed in classrooms violated the separation of church and state.
