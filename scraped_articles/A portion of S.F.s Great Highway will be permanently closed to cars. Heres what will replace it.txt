A portion of S.F.’s Great Highway will be permanently closed to cars. Here’s what will replace it
-----
A mile-long portion of San Francisco's Great Highway is set to close to car traffic for good by early 2026, as part of a project to protect the city's western shore from rising sea levels.City officials have long conceded that chronic and worsening coastal erosion would force the ultimate closure of what's known as the Great Highway Extension between Sloat and Skyline boulevards. The roadway has been pegged for closure by the city since 2012.
