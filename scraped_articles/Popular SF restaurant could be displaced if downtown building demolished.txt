Popular SF restaurant could be displaced if downtown building demolished
-----
A permit was filed with the San Francisco Planning Department Wednesday for the demolition of 500 Brannan St., a one-story building that currently houses the 13-year-old restaurant Marlowe.The structure proposed to replace it is a five-story, mixed-use office and retail building with a restaurant tenant on the ground floor and a usable rooftop deck. The new restaurant would span 9,931 square feet and include additional basement kitchen space.
