This Bay Area county just adopted a controversial tool to force homeless people into shelter
-----
San Mateo County approved a controversial proposal Tuesday that aims to "encourage" homeless residents at illegal encampments to accept shelter or be charged with a misdemeanor.The county Board of Supervisors unanimously OKed the "Hopeful Horizons" ordinance, which would allow authorities to charge people living in encampments on public property with a misdemeanor after a person is given two written warnings and if a person declines shelter twice, the county said.
