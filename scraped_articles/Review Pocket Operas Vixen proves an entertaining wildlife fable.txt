Review: Pocket Opera’s ‘Vixen’ proves an entertaining wildlife fable
-----
There aren't many operas in the standard repertoire as hard to get your head around as Leos Janácek's 1924 comic fable "The Cunning Little Vixen." The music makes luscious, beautiful sense, but what the heck is actually going on here?There are animals of all kinds — forest creatures, barnyard livestock and humans — and they interact freely, as though barriers of species and language were merely fictions. OK, that's pretty standard for fairy tales.
