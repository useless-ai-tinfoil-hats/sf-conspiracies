Nonprofits accuse S.F. firm of squeezing them out of historic building to make room for office space
-----
At a time when city officials are desperate to breathe life into downtown San Francisco by filling empty buildings with artists, nonprofits and small businesses, the tenants of a historic cultural and artistic hub in the city's Mission District face a different problem: a landlord seemingly determined to get rid of them.The fight is taking place inside the Redstone Building, an 109-year-old brick building at the corner of 16th and Capp streets.
