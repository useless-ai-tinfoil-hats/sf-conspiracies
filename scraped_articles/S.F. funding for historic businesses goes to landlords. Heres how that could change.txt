A San Francisco program to support historic businesses could change the way it distributes about $1 million in grants each year, modifying a system that currently favors landlords.At a meeting of the city's Small Business Commission Monday, the Legacy Business Program's manager, Richard Kurylo, proposed changes that would require landlords to share the grants with their legacy tenants. He cited recent reporting by the San Francisco Chronicle as "inspiration."
