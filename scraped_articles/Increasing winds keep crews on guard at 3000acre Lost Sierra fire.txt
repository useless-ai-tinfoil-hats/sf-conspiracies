Increasing winds keep crews on guard at 3,000-acre Lost Sierra fire
-----
The Gold Complex fire in Plumas National Forest has grown to more than 3,000 acres, and while crews have made significant progress building lines, containment remains low. Fire officials remain cautious as a red flag warning went into effect Thursday afternoon.The large column of smoke that rose over the Lost Sierra communities of Clio, Graeagle and Blairsden earlier this week has dissipated. However, winds are increasing into the afternoon. And there's still a lot of unburned vegetation
