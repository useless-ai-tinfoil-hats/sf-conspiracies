BART inspector general finds employees cost transit agency thousands in time theft
-----
BART's inspector general found three of the transit agency's employees recently committed time theft by clocking in for work and collecting pay while spending most of their time at home not working.According to the Office of the Inspector General's investigation, the employees, who were not approved for remote work, claimed to work 10-hour in-person shifts. The employees reported for their in-person assignments, "for brief periods," before returning home for most of their shifts.
