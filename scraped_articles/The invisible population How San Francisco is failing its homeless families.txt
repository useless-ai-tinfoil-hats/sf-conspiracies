‘The invisible population’: How San Francisco is failing its homeless families
-----
Every night at 7 p.m., families lug their belongings to the gate of Buena Vista Horace Mann, a K-8 public school in the Mission District, looking for a safe place to get a meal and spend the night. There are 69 cots and mats available, case workers on site, showers and hot dinners. In the five years the shelter has been open, 1,300 people have spent a night there, and more than 400 families have transitioned into stable housing.
