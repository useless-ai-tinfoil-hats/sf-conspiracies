Families in a San Francisco ‘food desert’ can now shop for free at a city-run grocery
-----
San Francisco has opened its first-ever city-funded community market, providing free groceries to eligible families to help address food insecurity in a neighborhood classified as a "food desert."The D10 Community Market in San Francisco's Bayview-Hunters Point district opened on June 5 at 5030 Third St. in a 4,000-square-foot building that had been empty for nearly a decade.
