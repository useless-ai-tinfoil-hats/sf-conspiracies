4.5-magnitude earthquake shakes Mendocino County
-----
A 4.5 magnitude earthquake shook Mendocino County in Northern California on Friday evening, according to the United States Geological Survey.The epicenter of the quake, which occurred shortly before 9 p.m., was about three miles southwest of the unincorporated community of Laytonville, which is in the north-central part of the county.
"It was very brief 3-5 seconds, some light shaking is all," a woman located in Fort Bragg told the Chronicle.
There were no immediate reports
