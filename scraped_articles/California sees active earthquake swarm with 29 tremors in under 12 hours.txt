California sees 'active earthquake swarm' with 29 tremors in under 12 hours
-----
A series of earthquakes — the biggest a tremor with a preliminary magnitude of 4.8 — hit El Centro, Calif., Monday morning and gave Imperial and San Diego counties a load of little shakes, according to the U.S. Geological Survey. The tremors just kept coming as the day continued.All of this rumbling in an area of California near the Mexico border is the result of "an active earthquake swarm," said Stephen DeLong, a supervisory research geologist with the USGS.
