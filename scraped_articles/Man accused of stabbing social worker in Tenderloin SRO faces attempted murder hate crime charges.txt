Man accused of stabbing social worker in Tenderloin SRO faces attempted murder, hate crime charges
-----
A San Francisco man accused of stabbing a social worker at a permanent supportive housing facility in the Tenderloin neighborhood is facing anti-gay hate crime and attempted murder charges, the district attorney's office said Thursday.Oscar Chatman, 27, was charged with making anti-LGBTQ threats that are considered hate crimes, prosecutors said. Chatman was also charged with premeditated attempted murder, assault with a deadly weapon, vandalism and burglary for the Sept. 13 stabbing.
