Review: ‘House of Ninjas’ modernizes the Japanese action genre with generic Netflix touches but pretty good fights
-----
"House of Ninjas" brings modern family twists to the Japanese martial arts genre. It includes doses of political intrigue, historical context, religious fanaticism, chaste romance and misguided broad comedy.Based on a concept by star Kento Kaku ("From Today, It's My Turn") and fleshed out by writer/showrunner Dave Boyle, the eight-episode Netflix series is at its best during action scenes.
