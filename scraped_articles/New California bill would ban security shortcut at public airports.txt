New California bill would ban security shortcut at public airports
-----
LATEST April 24, 1 p.m. A bill that sought to limit line-cutting services at airports passed out of the state Senate Transportation Committee along an 8-4 vote Tuesday, but not without significant changes.Senate Bill 1372 originally sought to ban new agreements between airports and third-party vendors such as Clear Secure Inc., which allows subscribers who pay an annual fee to skip ahead of other passengers to the front of existing security lanes.
