California becomes first state to create a Fast Food Council for fast food workers
-----
California has a newly appointed Fast Food Council ahead of a state law that will increase the hourly pay for fast food workers to $20 on April 1.On Friday, Gov. Gavin Newsom appointed seven members to join the nine-member Fast Food Council, which was created to help set rules for franchise owners and restaurant chain representatives and working conditions for the state's nearly 500,000 fast food workers, according to CalMatters. The council is expected to start meeting by March 15.
