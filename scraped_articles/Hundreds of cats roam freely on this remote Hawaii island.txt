Hundreds of cats roam freely on this remote Hawaii island
-----
Somehow, the cats survived multiple bombings. The Hawaii island of Kahoolawe lies mostly uninhabited in clear view from Maui, just 7 miles away. The small, 45-square-mile island is sacred and culturally significant to Native Hawaiians — it's dedicated to Kanaloa, the god of the ocean — but many people know it by the nickname, "The Target Isle."For four decades, Kahoolawe was used for target practice and as a training ground by the U.S. military.
