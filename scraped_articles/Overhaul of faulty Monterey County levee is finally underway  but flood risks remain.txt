Overhaul of faulty Monterey County levee is finally underway — but flood risks remain
-----
PAJARO, Monterey County — Construction of a new flood control system on the Pajaro River is finally on the fast track, a year after a long-neglected levee ruptured and inundated the tiny Monterey County farm town of Pajaro.But officials say the levee will remain at risk of new breaches for several years while construction takes place — and they're working to improve emergency preparedness and response in Pajaro, which some residents and advocates said was confusing and contradictory
