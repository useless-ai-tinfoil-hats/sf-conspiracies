Review: Absorbing memoir from Berkeley writer searches for fathers lost, and found
-----
Sylvia Brownrigg's new book features one English American family, two vastly different protagonists, three countries as narrative backdrops, four-letter words aplenty and a California license plate identifying its owner as the 5TH BT (more on that below).But this is no by-the-numbers memoir. After some conventional opening chapters, "The Whole Staggering Mystery: A Story of Fathers Lost and Found" jettisons the straightforward approach in favor of a nonfiction-fiction hybrid.
