Man who was first fentanyl murder conviction in Calif. gets prison sentence
-----
A 22-year-old man who received California's first murder conviction in connection with a fatal fentanyl overdose was sentenced to 15 years to life in prison on Tuesday, the Placer County District Attorney's Office said.Nathaniel Evan Cabacungan, 22, pleaded guilty on July 12 to second-degree murder after providing counterfeit Percocet pills laced with a lethal doses of fentanyl to a 15-year-old Roseville girl. Jewels Wolf died on June 21, 2022, shortly after consuming pills.
