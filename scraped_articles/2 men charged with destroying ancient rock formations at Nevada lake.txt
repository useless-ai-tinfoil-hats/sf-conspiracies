2 men charged with destroying ancient rock formations at Nevada lake
-----
Two men were charged on Aug. 23 with toppling ancient rock formations at Lake Mead National Recreation Area in Nevada, according to a federal indictment.The men from Henderson, Nevada, ages 31 and 37, were caught on video on April 7 pushing the massive pieces of sandstone over a cliff and onto the ground below, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of Nevada said in a news release. They were on the Redstone Dune Trail, a 1.1-mile round-trip hike in the national recreation area
