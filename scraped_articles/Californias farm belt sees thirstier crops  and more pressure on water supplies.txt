California’s farm belt sees thirstier crops — and more pressure on water supplies
-----
Climate change is driving up the thirst of crops significantly in California's San Joaquin Valley, new research shows, adding to the critical water challenges faced by one of the world's leading agricultural regions.The total water demand of orchards, vineyards and row crops in the area is up 4.4% over the past decade compared with the prior 30 years because of hotter, drier conditions, and it's likely to continue growing, according to a federally funded study published this week.
