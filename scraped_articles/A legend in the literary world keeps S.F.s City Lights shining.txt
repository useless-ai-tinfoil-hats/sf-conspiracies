‘A legend in the literary world’ keeps S.F.’s City Lights shining
-----
It sounds like the kind of legend that gathers fictional flourishes over time, but the details check out. In 1970, when he was a 20-year-old student at what was then called San Francisco State College, Paul Yamazaki joined a strike to demand the establishment of a Black studies department and a School of Ethnic Studies. In the streets, stopping traffic, he was arrested.The punishment: two concurrent six-month sentences.
