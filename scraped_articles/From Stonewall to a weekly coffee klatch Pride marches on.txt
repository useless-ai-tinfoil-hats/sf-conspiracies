From Stonewall to a weekly coffee klatch, Pride marches on
-----
New York's Stonewall Inn was not always the Stonewall Inn. In the 1840s, the building at 53 Christopher St. was a horse stable. In the 1930s it became a speakeasy. On March 18, 1967, it opened as a gay bar, the only one in the city to allow open dancing.
