Bachelorette parties are descending onto Napa — whether wineries like it or not
-----
It was 10:15 a.m. and a group of women pulled up to their six-hour Napa Wine Train tour wearing sashes and matching outfits. It was obviously a bachelorette party — and they were already inebriated."We knew we had our hands full right from the beginning of the day," recalled Nathan Davis, the Wine Train's general manager.
The party had to be asked repeatedly to quiet down. At one winery stop, they opted out of the tasting, took their shoes off and laid down in a field of grass
