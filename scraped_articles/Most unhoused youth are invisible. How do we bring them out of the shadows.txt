Most unhoused youth are invisible. How do we bring them out of the shadows?
-----
As a kid, I thought being invisible was a superpower. Being a Navy brat who moved every two years meant my social life had to start from scratch with every move. But the older I got, the harder it became to make friends and fit in, so I did my best to just blend in and not draw attention to myself.It was a useful survival skill for me. But for young people experiencing homelessness, being invisible makes their lives far worse.
