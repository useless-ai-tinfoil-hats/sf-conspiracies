The latest proposal to fix S.F.’s housing slump: Slash the city’s transfer tax by half
-----
In an effort to rev up San Francisco's housing production engine, Supervisor Ahsha Safaí is introducing legislation that would cut the city's transfer tax in half for projects that are financed, at least in part, by union pension funds.The proposal would reduce the transfer tax from 6% to 3% for housing developments that include at least $25 million in investment from union pension funds, which invest on behalf of retired workers.
