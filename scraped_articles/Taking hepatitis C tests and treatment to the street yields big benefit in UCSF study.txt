Taking hepatitis C tests and treatment to the street yields big benefit in UCSF study
-----
In July 2020, when many medical centers were slashing testing for non-COVID conditions, UCSF associate professor of epidemiology Meghan Morris and her colleagues rented a storefront in San Francisco's South of Market neighborhood to do just the opposite.Over the next 15 months, they conducted a study testing nearly 500 people for hepatitis C, a potentially deadly but treatable viral infection that can cause cirrhosis or liver failure.
