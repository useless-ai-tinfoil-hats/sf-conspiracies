California initiates first-ever crackdown on groundwater pumping by farms
-----
Despite pleas from farmers, California water regulators on Tuesday took the extraordinary step of assuming oversight of groundwater pumping in a swath of the state's agricultural heartland, a first-of-its-kind move intended to shore up diminished groundwater reserves.The Tulare Lake subbasin, a hydrological region between Fresno and Bakersfield that is now officially on "probation," has seen its aquifers run low, wells run dry and land sink as a result of overpumping.
