Death Valley records hottest month ever during recent Calif. heat wave
-----
During a scorching heat wave that blasted California in July, Death Valley recorded its hottest month ever.According to the National Weather Service's preliminary data, the average daily temperature for July was 108.5 degrees Fahrenheit, which beat the national park's previous record of 108.1 degrees in 2018, Brian Planz, a meteorologist with the weather service, told SFGATE.
