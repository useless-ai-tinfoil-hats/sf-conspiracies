Charts show how many Native American remains still need to be returned in California
-----
It's been more than 30 years since the U.S. Congress acknowledged that Native ancestral remains and cultural artifacts first and foremost belong to the indigenous people of their origin. However, since the law's passing in the 1990s, hundreds of thousands of human remains and culturally significant objects have yet to be returned to their descendants.An analysis of publicly available data under the Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act shows that California is a hotspot
