S.F. has 1,000 empty units for the homeless. One supervisor wants to cut vacancy in half
-----
Supervisor Dean Preston is urging San Francisco's Department of Homelessness and Supportive Housing to fill 500 vacant units for formerly homeless people within 90 days and to pledge to keep its vacancy rate below 5% to deal with the epidemic of unhoused people on city streets.San Francisco has an estimated 1,002 empty permanent supportive housing units, or about 10% its stock of about 10,000 units, according to Preston's office, with about 774 available for referral and 288 "offline"
