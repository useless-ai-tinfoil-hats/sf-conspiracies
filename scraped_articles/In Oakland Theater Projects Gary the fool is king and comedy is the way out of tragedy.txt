In Oakland Theater Project’s “Gary,” the fool is king, and comedy is the way out of tragedy
-----
In 1964, the scholar Jan Kott wrote that Shakespeare's "Macbeth" is animated by one dream: "a murder that will break the murder cycle, will be the way out of nightmare." Many protagonists in his other plays suffer from the same illusion, that the murders they commit will be special somehow, and no one else will ever kill a king again.In "Gary: A Sequel to Titus Andronicus," playwright Taylor Mac envisions another way to interrupt the churn of violence.
