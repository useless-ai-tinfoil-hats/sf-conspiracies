‘Scared to death’: S.F. librarians call for security guards at every branch
-----
When Nicole Termini Germain began her career as a librarian more than 30 years ago, she was never taught how to de-escalate dangerous situations. Yet, two years ago she said she found herself standing between a half-dressed man wielding a sharp letter opener and a group of preschoolers."I was scared to death," Germain said Tuesday, recounting the incident. "But as branch manager and children's librarian, I have to protect everyone. The burden falls on me every time."
